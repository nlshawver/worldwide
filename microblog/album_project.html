2022 album project

In an effort to essentially force myself to listen to more music in a more cohesive and rigid way, I've set out an objective of listening to one album a day for the entirety of 2022. I have the list done already, with everything picked out in advance between december 29th and 31st. So while any recommendations won't be included (unless I completely rearrange that list), I will appreciate the feedback, and will likely use it as an excuse to make a video. What I am saying is that either way, please give me more music to listen to :)

Anyways, I have below all of the information about this that you'd like, from the last album I listened to, the upcoming week's worth of albums, and of course the archive of already-listened-to albums (with links; images are too big). And just in case you don't have them, my snapchat and instagram account names are available via my contact page.

    mini notes for voodoo

    • this instrumental, that slowly keeps pulling back the curtain on certain instruments is already compelling and interesting as all hell; I know what the best track is going to be already, but I need the affirmation that the build-up is worth it
      • those two verses are already great signs, the staggered delivery (and the very characteristic timbre from d'angelo) is something that’s already likable in a confident sort of way :)
      • questlove, dj premier, and q-tip are all national treasures c:
      • that repeated outro of “play on, play on” is such a head-nodder; the smooth delivery (and just everything that surrounds it to make it feel like the perfect setting and atmosphere) is effortlessly engaging :D
    • that introduction into devil’s pie is such a foggy setting to lead into; and my god everything about this track is hauntingly appealing
      • it’s a testament to its meaning the way it’s arranged :oo
      • “Right or wrong, do or die / Only vengeance could pacify” damnn, I love a lot of the lyricism on this track, but that stuck out to me the most ^^
    • was not expecting the only (listed/main) features here to be redman and method man, but especially the latter is incredible in the foreground of left & right :)
      • honestly just a really great cut; it seems to not have as direct a core meaning as the previous two tracks, but a more general moment, laden in syrupy appeal c:
    • and that transition into the line :oo
      • among the other fades between tracks too of course- but that was particularly good going from method man’s lyricism, right into this more laidback and personable style, filled with both risk aversion and intense attachment to that risk ^^
    • damn, and again with these thematic ties to previous tracks; going from a track about literally holding the line of logic and reality, into a commending of love in general, but specifically for d’angelo’s newborn son… it’s intense—and even if the tracks feel similar, it’s an amazing connection that feels absolutely potent :D
      • beautiful ending right into chicken grease; i love you questlove :)
    • the fact that chicken grease is in reference to (and in effect an ode to) both prince and george clinton fits perfectly; it’s yet again a fantastic neo soul anthem of unadulterated bliss c:
    • god damn the theme of lost loves and past relationships on one mo’gin is perfect; genuinely one of the best cuts here so far, in a sea of gold :D
      • it’s just such a smooth experience, and the bait and switch outro is amazing too c:
    • I don’t have to tell you that the path from requited love to feeling under a spell from your ex in this tracklist, now with the root, is a great connection; it’s amazing, and I love everything about it :))
      • that reversed guitar is also just stunning in this instrumental backdrop- such a nice touch to cap off the midsection of this ethereal yet cold track :oo
      • and the extended chorus outro… damn c:
    • god, both the horns and guitar-work on spanish joint are done and applied so well here; it might be the most transitionary track in the runtime of this record, but it serves its place well, and sounds amazing going through its crevasses c:
      • go on to the chorus again ;)
    • ooh that small, one-beat break in feel like makin’ love c: such a great moment to have sprinkled into this obviously sensual ballad :)
      • and while it does drag out a bit more than other tracks here… it gives you enough time…
      • god it’s so mf appealing, can’t think of anything else rn (:
    • “Still long for happiness / Not promised to the plenty” god that end to the first verse of greatdayndamornin’ is just fantastic :D
      • this mf instrumental is so good; the way it sometimes blends, sometimes shifts around d’angelo’s voice is amazing, and the small complexity of it balances and mellows it out well :)
    • booty is a nice end to that medley :)
    • still wasn’t prepared for it… already tearing up after the journey this record has already been—
    • …give me a second…
    • yea I teared up, and felt shivers each and every time d’angelo’s voice rocketed through, demanding to be cherished for its pure frenzy of enchantment and yearning
      • what a fucking song; one of few tracks that genuinely deserves more praise, despite already being loved for how expressive its fervor is…
    • and while I wasn’t expecting to come away from that masterpiece into something better, africa truly is a fantastic closer as well; those rumbling, low bass hits, and the love now for an entire continent, culture, and state of mind is absolutely beautiful
    • what an album
