2022 album project

In an effort to essentially force myself to listen to more music in a more cohesive and rigid way, I've set out an objective of listening to one album a day for the entirety of 2022. I have the list done already, with everything picked out in advance between december 29th and 31st. So while any recommendations won't be included (unless I completely rearrange that list), I will appreciate the feedback, and will likely use it as an excuse to make a video. What I am saying is that either way, please give me more music to listen to :)

Anyways, I have below all of the information about this that you'd like, from the last album I listened to, the upcoming week's worth of albums, and of course the archive of already-listened-to albums (with links; images are too big). And just in case you don't have them, my snapchat and instagram account names are available via my contact page.

    mini notes for igor

    • thx moment :o
      • in all seriousness though, what an absolutely incredible introduction to this LP :O
      • that insane beat, the piano, the strained vocals... everything combines together beautifully :))
    • perfect transition into the obviously most iconic song of the album- I've heard "true fans" say it's not as good... but come on, this is insanely good :D
      • tyler's pitched vocals are honestly a really great part of this record, and contribute rly nicely to the aesthetic choices on the album :)
      • carti not bad either ^^
      • outro is one of the best parts too, lovely stuff :o
      • ~bass~
    • damn, another great transition; the crackles are a nice addition too :)
      • the theme of the track and the way tyler acts over this incessantly catchy beat is almost playful- the little moment before the long instrumental is lovely
      • the pause right back into the female vocalist (bodiono nkono?) is amazing :O
    • hehe the tiny interlude into (and including) running out of time is rly soothing almost ^^
    • letting the track grow and develop thematically with tyler's super-pitched vocals this time- before getting into the subtly punchy beat and longer vocal lines is vv good :)
      • god I love the panning back and forth
    • DAMN that start to new magic wand :OO
      • what an insane journey of a track; the story of him struggling with his partner is poignant and depraved, but it feels genuine and emotional :o
    • a boy is a gun is a classic too; the little vocal sample behind tyler's smooth verses and choruses ties this together amazingly imo ^
      • the conflict (and self-reference) is a huge perk to this cut, it's honestly, weirdly moving and significant ^^
    • was not expecting ye on puppet, but the ties to the theme and purpose of this cut in the tracklist is great too- the beat and everything wrapped up beautifully here too :oo
    • the huge difference between the insanely distorted synths behind tyler's rapping, and the honestly beautiful, normal instrumental- is incredible
      • didn't expect slowthai either but his presence is also a gift- OH MY GOD the laughing :OOO
      • this cut is absolutely ridiculous and god I fucking love it...:oooo
      • the deep breaths...
      • letting go, or being okay with it...?
    • the fact that all of the vocals on pt 1 of gone gone / thank you is tyler is rly damn impressive honestly- this first part is a bit of a good moment, of self-realization and a come down from the seemed unadulterated rage of the last track ^
      • mf ceelo green... my god dude :)
      • and wow, the part where is full voice comes through is amazing (thank u jerrod carmichael btw) :O
      • thank you is a really beautiful part of the track, and a clear sort of feeling to cap this whole thing off :o
    • the rough production of i don't love you anymore is thematically genius- it might be a bit surface level, but with the strained chaos of the chorus and vocal sections as a whole, I think it adds a lot of genuine, creative sensationalism to the cut :)
    • an al green sample, jack white on guitar, and a pharrell cameo? god damn dude, what the fuck
      • the careful, driving instrumental, along with tyler's close-to-normal voice tying this record up into a bow... this whole thing is just fantastically intoxicating :ooo
      • oh my god — that comeback...
      • speechless
