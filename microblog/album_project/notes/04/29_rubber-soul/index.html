mini notes for rubber soul

  • it's a really simple song, but I love the instrumental of drive my car; it's layered and a intriguing for such a theme :)
  • norwegian wood is genuinely my favorite beatles song ever; it's such an absolutely beautiful track with the sitar, and the layered instrumental in each ear compliments the style wonderfully :D
    • the lyrics are interesting as well, and lennon's vocals come off really smoothly ^^
  • I can think of you won't see me as just a basic sort of folk rock track for the beatles, but it's still somehow a standout moment for me; I'm really liking this so far :)
  • nowhere man is probably the weakest though lol
    • the lyrics are still interesting, but I'm not as captivated or intrigued by the instrumental
  • think for yourself is just like you won't see me; it's not super complex, but the lyrics and the way the instrumental feels still makes it a lovely track ^^
  • the way the word starts :o
    • not my favorite as it goes along, but not bad
  • oooooh shit I know michelle :OOO
    • that track is so good with the mixing into french and the very supple bass(?) that goes along in the background and ending; absolutely wonderful track :D
  • wow, what goes on didn't start great for me but grew on me very quickly :)
    • the way it ends was really damn good too
  • girl is a pretty nice track, and the meaning is... right there, it's obvious
  • i'm looking through you I think is a more compelling continuous moment from girl; it's more masked and foggy in its interesting elements :)
  • in my life is a beautiful little track (and the way it starts is lovely); one of my more favorite and calmer tracks so far :)
  • I think wait and if I needed someone go hand in hand in the tracklist, and both are pretty interesting, great ways to keep the LP going
  • and while I get the point of run for your life, I can't help but think it could've been ended better... but I guess I'm not the beatles (:
