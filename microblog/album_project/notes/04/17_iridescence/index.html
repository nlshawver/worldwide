mini notes for iridescence

  • jesus, same immediate energy on here as saturation III; that crunchy beat is absolutely infectious :O
    • all the verses through this thing are amazing, but kevin abstract and merlyn wood’s especially are incredible :o
  • the transition and thematic shift into thug life is great too, it feels so natural and kinda beautiful with that piano in the backdrop ^
  • and right back into it with berlin :O
    • holy SHIT- that bearface chorus with that hugely industrial instrumental is a face-melted with how insane it gets :D
    • the joba verse at the end feels a bit off, but the calm aesthetic of his verse offsets how insanely crunchy this song is
  • something about him is a nice interlude moment, a bit of a respite in a way- and while I like where the cash at in terms of its callbacks to cash on saturation, it’s just kind of alright to me (certainly good tho, just not my favorite)
  • the way it goes straight into the strings of weight is really damn good though :O
    • abstract’s verse is a great match with that somber sort of energy, and the scratches after are such a huge moment :o
    • joba & dom’s verses too :D
    • just a lovely cut in the midst of this wild journey; I love the self-reflection and the depths of emotion ^
  • wow… district is such a huge statement, and while it’s playful at times in the effects and intonations, the main piece as a whole just feels absolutely shuddering in its stature :O
  • loophole is a lovely interlude too :)
  • damn, tape is similar to district for me- I’m blown away by the lyrics and the way each member comes across and shows themselves on the track, even if the beat isn’t my absolute favorite ^
    • yea tape is definitely one of my favorites on the record, the strings to end it off and just the way it transcribes its thematic message is incredible :)
  • holy fuck… blown away by joba on j’ouvert :OOOO
    • tape into this is absolutely insane, I don’t know how this is even possible; merlyn on the third verse(?) is incredible too, and bearface’s contributions are lovely too :)
  • that transition :o
  • oh my god, I thought honey started off well with abstract & wood again, but that ending minute with the huge synth leads; absolutely incredible :D
  • god damn dom :OOOOOO
    • I’m not a huge fan of the way vivid fits into this tracklist I think, but jesus christ if his verse isn’t incredible alongside matt on the start :D
  • while I don’t think I’m blown away or anything by san marcos, the somber and reflective tone that it splits away into is a great moment for the album I think; the verses have a great amount of meaning and significance to them too :o
    • the guitar on the start just set me off I think, not a huge fan for that
  • wow, both abstract and bearface on tonya are lovingly crafted little moments- their emotive nature about past events and especially ameer on bear’s verse elevates this track a lot ^
    • oh my god that ending is beautiful :OO
  • joba and kevin on fabric are both beautiful- the verse from abstract and half-bridge that joba delivers are lovely
    • I am… conflicted about that ending though
    • it feels so disjointed yet calculated, I just don’t know how it feels as the ending to such an LP ^^
