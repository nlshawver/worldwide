mini notes for good news for people who love bad news

  • I wasn't initially confident of the style that came through on the first real cut, the world at large, but it turned into a nice little ballad by the end; not as iconic or interesting as the other record I reviewed from them (the lonesome crowded west), but not bad at all
  • and then float on is a classic, I absolutely love this track :D
    • that transition is insanely well done too, that was incredible :OO
    • it is kinda weird hearing isaac brock appreciate these little moments in life; from what I gather this is a rare moment in their discography :o
  • ocean breathes salty was a relatively nice follow-up, but it isn’t nearly as memorable as the previous two cuts imo 6
  • bury me with it has a bit more to it, and I like the way it divulges a bit at around the midway point into chaos… but I honestly just can’t connect a ton with the vocals rn—I will hold my breath for later in the LP though of course
  • okay dance hall is just… idek, this is just not good to me
    • I’m gonna try and ignore that
  • alright alright, I love the casual honesty and story-telling of bukowski though, it’s way more interesting that the past two(ish) tracks, and I love the instrumental style too :)
    • the ending is interesting too, with the deeper inflection of brock’s voice coming through really well ^
  • dude… okay this album is incredibly flawed, the two tracks that bookend bukowski just make no sense in terms of, you know, album cohesion :/
    • I fear the ability for this album to salvage those low points
  • ooh okay, the view is really damn good; I might like the general aesthetic of it more than bukowski, but both are wonderful moments (comparatively)
    • and wth the start of that second verse is amazing :D
    • everything that follows it is as well is just as intriguing, and a wonderful alt rock abode :)
  • satin in a coffin is a nice, rockist continuation of the aesthetic prior, and I like the way it carries itself ^^
    • my only fear is that it (and the following tracks) cannot make up for the chaos of this record
  • and wow, I am genuinely loving the introspective notes and calm, subtle instrumentation on blame it on the tetons… it feels vulnerable and it’s a valuable experience for the record to have :O
  • black cadillacs is the most destructive sounding track here, and unlike previously aggressive cuts, this cut has merit in its sound and cohesion in that underlying themes its getting across; this backend is really damn great so far :)
  • one chance explodes again with valuable, intriguing passages of alt rock bliss, and I love the back and forth that seems to go on with the energy level
    • that little break that goes into brock’s distorted vocals is ear candy :))
    • that and the slower, downtempo ending was a great way to lead into the final cut ^
  • the flaming lips bit at the start was a great little cameo—and I absolutely love the commentary that brock proclaims in a straightforward manner
    • it’s a creeping, weird track that has a lot of merit behind what brock’s saying; the instrumental behind him is absolutely lovely too, the staggering drums and weird, meshing harmonies are a great instrumental comparison that allows the message of this LP to end on a purposefully full-of-real-life experience
