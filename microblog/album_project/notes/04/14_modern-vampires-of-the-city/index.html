mini notes for modern vampires of the city

  • this instrumental and the general aesthetic of this so far is really nice; big fan :)
    • honestly really beautiful, interesting start… I think I have almost negatively boring thoughts about vampire weekend’s first two albums at this point, but I hope this is different
    • atmospheric noises :o
  • great transition into unbelievers, still keeping up this great intonation to this record that I really like :)
    • that chorus is satisfying as hell, lovely stuff; holy shit that bass :D
    • even better than the intro, this is truly great so far ^
  • step is a great, typical song for vampire weekend; it pretty much sums up everything that sounds exactly like VW
    • really somber, great ending to that track… but I feel like that feeling is cut short really quickly by the next track in a kinda harsh way :/
  • damn that voice effect on diane young is not that appealing or good at all… which sucks cause I think the rest of the instrumental is actually pretty good
  • I like don’t lie a good amount tho, it feels wonderfully chipper and still unique at the same time; the deeper voice inflection later on is a fantastic touch :))
  • wow… hanna hunt is a great little number, it feels both personable and specifically important ^
  • everlasting arms is another beautiful cut; it's like something to completely fit a comfortable rainy day, and the way it fits that mold is perfect :D
  • damn and the playful nature of finger back is incredibly infectious... maybe the earlier midsection of this record was a bit lackluster, but this is lovely :)
  • haha the chaos of worship you is amazing- perfect summer sort of cut, and the actual words he's speeding through fit that vibe well too ^^
    • that teched out synth break goes over rly damn well, esp with that drum beat that explodes in after that bass note descent :O
  • ya hey is another perfectly VW track; every element in the vocalizations and instrumental are carefully cut in that same consistent cloth of articulation :)
  • that creepy atmosphere and blown out drum section are such a dark turn into hudson; it's surprisingly interesting in its operation and feels like a crazy experience ^
    • this back end of the record is incredible, genuinely- to think I almost wrote off this record feels ridiculous at this point, similar to currents yesterday
  • it's a simple and very brief cut at the end... but I love how young lion fits- the piano felt so different and distinct from what hudson put you into, and the lyrics from ezra come through in a very meaningful way :)
