mini notes for the low end theory

  • wow what a start to this album, that beat behind q-tip’s rhymes is absolutely infectious, and all the bars are poetically inclined with some great introspective elements :)
    • absolutely lovely start ^
  • they do call him dr. pepper :)
    • OOOH both phife’s verse and q-tip’s verse following right after are so smooth, and the subtle and offset cadence of them is so satisfying :D
    • god buggin’ out is just an absolute treat from start to finish :))
  • I love that out-tide line, so spry—and god this beat on rap promoter is an earworm :O
    • great little ending and transition to…
  • that beat on butter :O
    • I don’t want to be that guy, but this beat is smooth as— nvm
    • oh okay phife dawg said it so I’m good—but holy shit that line where he goes through the women’s names and how he treated them… god damn that’s crisp as hell :D
  • verses from the abstract is a nice little downtempo track, more tired than the last but never sleepy or uninteresting; some of the bars here are the most introspective yet, like “The thing that men and women need to do is stick together / Progressions can’t be made if we’re separate forever”
    • that upright bass is such a fantastic choice for this track :)
    • I also love all the references to rappers (artists) that q-tip respects, like big daddy kane, busta rhymes, etc.
  • god damn, show business is an absolute slam, that beat and quick sample under these high-profile and exciting rhymes is stunning :D
    • that lord jamar verse is a great little feature, and going right back into phife’s verse is lovely :))
    • sadat x is really damn good too, but HOLY diamond d’s verse is fantastic over this beat :DD
    • one of my favorite moments on the record for sure, this whole thing is an earworm ^
  • I just have to say how much I love the song title “vibes and stuff” :)
    • it really fits the song though (ofc), all the verses straddle the line between playfully casual and still overtly serious in the provocative themes, like q-tip’s referencing of the little engine that could as a way to comment on kid’s gang culture ^
    • a very casual way to bring about these themes, incredibly compelling track :)
  • okay, I kinda like the commentary and general attitude on the infamous date rape- it’s definitely much more interesting in terms of its status than that of other misogynistic tracks of the time ^
    • good on them I guess, it’s still an incredibly compelling track ^^
  • GOD that beat on check the rhime :DD
    • all the call-and-response stuff is so satisfying and casual in how skilled it is; I love this so damn much :)
  • while everything is fair might be the most okay track so far, the commentary and verses on life in the city (nyc) is interesting and definitively personal throughout the runtime ^
  • great little transition to jazz (we’ve got) :)
    • green dolphin street is lovely for this cut ^
    • that dancehall type interpolation at the start of phife’s verse is a great little moment for a callback- and this whole track is so subtly interesting and almost somber in its intonation :)
    • I’m glad it has the runtime to stretch out and grow with these verses back and forth ^
  • skypager, somehow, while still having elements similar to all other tracks here, is still keeping things interesting over this 50 minute runtime :)
    • that funky beat behind it is a great way to separate the remaining tracks and give a little breath back to the listener (me)
    • you’re welcome ;)
  • all balls no cock?
    • that menage a trois set was incredible lol
    • these constant questions and clear connections being made is a great almost interlude… not my favorite, but it’s a useful moment for the record no doubt :)
  • scenario is an absolute romp, that instrumental is amazing :)
    • I may be less than blown away by charlie brown’s verse, but GOD dinco d is incredible on this track :D
    • busta rhymes is a classic as always with a gritty verse closing off this record (which I think is a nice moment, given how much tip was commenting on him earlier in the tracklist)
