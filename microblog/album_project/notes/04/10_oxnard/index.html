mini notes for oxnard

  • I was a bit chilled by the start; not much happens, but it seems like a good opener now that headlow is on
    • which, by the way, is a great, minimal soul cut that I think hits a pretty good nerve in my brain (especially with the bridge and chorus :)
    • okay that mf outro is completely ridiculous, wth
  • god that outro sucks because the next track is so damn good; that incredibly funky bass line and general harmony is amazing with both paak’s verses and kendrick’s feature :))
  • oooh woah… who r u? has such a minimal, punchy beat and general energy to it that—while not truly outstanding imo—is still shockingly catchy :)
  • honestly I don’t know what to think of 6 summers; part of me loves pieces of the verses and how they flow in and amongst themselves, but I don’t think the chorus itself is all that great… but that switch-up and the final minute are pretty damn good ^
  • the swagger in the flow and general interlude-type stylings of saviers road is a nice little detour… good stuff :)
  • ooh that switch in the middle of smile / petty was a great way to go about this sort of style; I can’t comment much on smile, but the bass line and intricacies of petty are really damn catchy ^
    • paak’s voice always sounds almost sounds adventurous and yet at the same time completely casual; great stuff :D
  • wow, those synth lines on mansa musa, over the most villainous I think I’ve ever heard paak… that dre feature goes over amazingly, and this whole track is insane :OO
  • damn same with this brother’s keeper with pusha t :O
    • this whole thing sounds so damn huge, I love the way push and paak trade throughout the track, and the sort of contrast each of them have against each other’s voices
    • AHHHH THE JASON DERULO LINE
    • that’s an amazing comedown too, the guitar line that comes in a more serious way into this track is a great choice :)
  • of course snoop is on here too lol; I don’t think I’m as blown away by the feature and some of the cut as a whole, but the last two minutes are lovely with the way paak stretches out and relaxes over this lovely beat :)
  • honestly… not a bad j cole feature; I might be a bit hard on him, but for the second last track on the (normal version of the) LP, I like it a lot more than I would’ve expected ^
    • it justifies its runtime too, with some great mellow sections and nice guitar work in the background :)
  • what an emotive way to cap off this record… I might not have loved q-tip’s verse as much, but both sections, dedicated to mac and phife, are just so lovingly crafted and hugely introspective
    • I can’t not love the dedication, love, and energy that made it onto that cut -<>-
