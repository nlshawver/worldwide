mini notes for ready to die

  • jesus what a chaotic intro, from curtis mayfield to crime to big plans :O
  • things done changed is a triumphant introduction to the sound of biggie, and my lord — even if it's a previously unknown track to me, the qualities of a master are all there :))
  • god, the boom bap beat behind biggie's inflection and proclamations on gimme the loot is my personal peak so far; it's so tough and huge in its stature :D
  • while I'm not as in love with machine gun funk, the beat is genuinely infectious as all hell :)
  • warning almost feels like an interlude in terms of its feel and general styling; it's a nice mini shift in terms of a lot of its thematic choices ^
  • the title track is an absolute masterpiece though- from the introspective bars all throughout the verses... it's a great peak to the first piece of this long, story-driven LP :O
  • part of me is a bit confused going from such interpersonal themes on the title track to the dick wagging of one more chance
    • and holy shit fuck me is exactly what it sounds like- it's an album sex scene
  • okay anyways... pleasantly surprised by the placement of method man on this ^
  • ignoring all those weird feelings from before... jesus christ juicy is such an incredible song :D
    • every bar is personable, and it all just meshes into itself so well
    • the super nintendo sega genesis line is a bit dumb, but god damn i love it :))
  • and wow- I wasn't sold on everyday struggle at first, but that beat is classic, and the last verse has some of my favorite lines in the whole album :)
  • ...damn— I thought me and my bitch was going to be a braggadocio's sort of thing, but god damn that last line was powerful :OO
  • holy shit and then it's right back to the infectious romp of big poppa- genuinely might be his best song ever, it's such a fantastic story laid on top of such an amazing isley brothers sample :DD
  • OOOOOH respect is by far the best deep cut here; this three track run is the best section of the whole record I think ^^^
  • friend of mine doesn't have nearly the swagger of a lot of other tracks here, but it's not a bad track at all, no worries ^
  • while I think I have similar feelings about unbelievable, the almost playful nature of the track in general is pretty infectious; it's a pretty good cut coming up on the end of this LP
  • —
  • I don't know what I expected from the final track, but sitting in my car, late at night, hearing all that come through... it's a haunting experience; there are no proper words that explain the feelings felt from person to person...
  • what a legendary album
