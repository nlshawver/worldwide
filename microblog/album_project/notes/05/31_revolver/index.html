mini notes for revolver

  • great little dig on tax collectors in britain as the start of this album (:
    • I don’t really have much else to say, great guitar work tho :o
  • oooh classic w eleanor rigby :D
    • honestly it was mainly the third verse that stuck out to me, but it had a good amount of interesting, kinda vague imagery that felt appealing or (at least) intriguing
  • okay and I’m starting to get the wide appeal in the experimental nature of the record with i’m only sleeping, especially with george’s backwards guitar solo :OO
    • genuinely a really cool moment, and the fact that it’s just fed backwards through a tape machine is incredible c:
  • jesus christ and love you to is such a wild cut, with just george singing and playing the sitar (with ringo being the only other one, playing the tambourine lol)
    • absolutely incredible track with the meaning behind the lyrics, and the inflection and flow of george’s voice :))
  • ooh I heard that little tape skip or reverb before the third verse of here, there and everywhere ;)
    • honestly this is just a really good ballad, it fits in surprisingly well after the previous cut, and it just has such a wonderful energy surrounding its instrumentation and lyrics ^
  • this mf song
    • yellow submarine is such a weird track to have here, especially given the fact that it’s just a children’s song, with an understated acid trip happening in the background?
    • I really don’t know, but it’s not my favorite beatles song ever for sure :/
  • oh my god another great instrumental on she said she said, both especially with the drums and guitar(s) :D
    • honestly just a really good closer to the first half of the record, I love it :)
  • okay I took a break with notes, but while I never recognized any of the tracks that took place on the second half, god the instrumentals were genuinely shocking at some points
  • iirc, good day sunshine, for no one, and tomorrow never knows were the tracks that really stuck out to me, either for their lyrical content, instrumentals, but usually both ^^
  • fantastic album, even if I don’t completely understand the huge appeal of it at times :)
