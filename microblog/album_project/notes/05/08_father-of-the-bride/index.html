mini notes for father of the bride

  • there’s no good way to describe what happens on the first track, but the trading of vocals back and forth, along with that stuttering, chopped up instrumental was a pretty good start to this LP :)
  • god and I don’t know what it is about the combination of the verses and that almost dreamy chorus, but harmony hall is such a lovely indie pop track; at the very least for my ears, it checks every single box for compelling instrumentation and a lovely crescendo :D
  • it was a simple little interlude, but the little “ehh…to an extent” at the end of bambina was the best part lol
  • while it’s not my favorite cut from them by a good distance, and that drum ending was a bit meh for me, this life was a great little track (especially with those subtle little additions to make it stand out…mainly the stuttering vocals)
  • i’m gonna be honest though, big blue just kinda came and went
  • the background of how long? is just a bit of okay instrumentation, that overblown kick and the mushiness of the rest is a bit wary; the vocals are good, but I’m not feeling a ton from this track
  • what the hell that orchestra hit caught me off guard lol
  • unbearably white is a nice, slightly understated cut; those wild strings on the instrumental break was really nice too :o
    • I’m not really feeling much from the verses, but this is only first listen; it’s still one of the better tracks so far ^
  • rich man was an interesting little interlude to follow, but I’m just not feeling much from a lot of these tracks so far :/
  • yea married in a gold rush was good at times, but never as interesting as I maybe made it out in my mind to be (given what I know about VW’s former works and what to expect)
  • my mistake isn’t much different…
    • I don’t know, I like that little split in the track, but it seems not to amount to much to the track; nothing happens because of it I guess is a good way to say it
    • I might not as looked that deeply into the lyrics as I suspected
  • woah was not expecting sympathy :o
    • that chorus does sound really great honestly, damn :O
    • ~ that bass line ~
    • maybe didn’t end as great as the rest of the track was to me, but not a bad exciting cut to have at this point in the record
  • this mf song
    • I think this is one of those songs that’s great to listen to…maybe once or twice
    • I don’t know, somehow a lot of this record just isn’t doing much for me right now; it’s not bad, just sprawling and constantly shifting, never giving me enough of something to latch onto
  • I guess unfortunately the most compelling part of flower moon to me is steve lacy’s deeper voice and the way it plays well with the backing instrumental; maybe I’m just in a bit of a rut with VW in terms of koenig’s voice and the incredibly consistent (yet sometimes much too similar) styling of his music
    • yea the lyrics here just aren’t incredibly spectacular, and koenig spreading out vocally on that chorus gets to me a bit :c
  • 2021 just passes by me too, not much that stuck out to me :/
  • …I can’t not be honest, we belong together is almost insufferable for me; there’s just nothing to it, it’s a boring ‘love song’ that just amounts to little on my brain
    • I won’t even remember this by the time we get to the end of this stretched-out album, even if the end is only ten minutes away
  • I think a lot of the singing on stranger comes across as really nice, but the cohesion of the actual meaning of the track is a bit…lacking in terms of that little something that makes a track feel unique, special, and meaningful (especially in an album context)
  • spring snow was nice, and it’s the most touching of the ‘interludes’ that come and go I think ^
  • I do kinda like the anti-climax on the chorus, and I can definitely understand this sort of introspection relating to koenig’s identity; I guess for the ending of this record, this is a not-half bad track
    • I’m just a bit at a loss for the album as a whole; this is a good track for the end :)
