mini notes for hybrid theory

  • god all of this nostalgia :D
    • papercut is an incredible intro to this album, and such an amazing statement for linkin park to begin their career with :O
    • god that thumping bass drum just gets to me :ooo
  • and right into this mf track with one step closer :O
    • it’s gonna be non-stop isn’t it? yea it’s gonna be non-stop
    • while this is their basic, intro song for anyone new, this is still a banger to this day; I don’t care about any of their later discog, this track and album is so mf iconic :D
  • with you might be one of the better deep cuts here, the layered vocals of the chorus tie it together really, really well; great stuff :)
  • points of authority is absolutely incredible too, every minute element of it (even if minute in this context is ear-crushing) is incessantly catchy, tactile, and crafted in perfection of a long-dormant genre
  • ah right, nevermind- crawling is the basic opener for this band
    • while I think this isn’t bad, it’s certainly not as good as young me thought it was; it’s a bit weird, wary, and not as endearing as previous or future cuts on this record imo
    • it still explodes with that chorus tho, my god :o
    • whatever that sound is right after the song is, as I will describe it as “a saw in a bottle” is so iconic to this record ^^
  • runaway is another rly good cut in the mix of great tracks, not much else to say tbh
  • my god, by myself starts with what sounds like huge industrial machinery slamming into itself; I can’t identify that sound specifically, but listening reveals it really well :O
    • the slower parts don’t do as much for me, but they seem vital for this track to survive, so I think it’s worthwhile ^
  • this mf song, in the end is another insanely popular track from this record… I think this one deserves it a bit more than crawling, its so incessantly catchy, nostalgic, stuck-in-its-time in a charming way, and even if that chorus is a bit overdone… god its too iconic :))
  • a place for my head has a really great cadence to it, with that stuttering, almost mangled guitar leads driving through the whole track; I think some of the lyrics aren’t nearly as impressive, but its still really damn good
    • the ending is hard as hell, but in terms of total merit… it’s just alright in relation to a lot of the rest of this record
  • the first half and style of forgotten is a good continuation of a place for my head, but its not a perfect follow-up at all imo
    • not bad at all though, I have to admit; that chorus is surprisingly melodic and well-formed
  • I think I have a distinctly personal connection to cure for the itch; it’s a bit of a ridiculous venture in the album, and the voiceover is a bit much… but I think I genuinely gained a lot of appreciation for hip-hop production and the way it could form and morph just from this track
    • I could tell it was a whole lot different from what I grew up with from my parents, and that style of sound-crushing and mixing, along with completely foreign, spritely sounds drew me in and never let go, even if I had forgotten about this track long ago
    • well, until today at least :)
    • it’s a lovely interlude on the album ^
  • pushing me away is a nice little closer, even if its not as iconoclastic or endearing as a few other tracks here; it’s a rigid, great ending with shinoda’s vocals backing up chester’s leads really well :)
    • yea that final note is lovely :oo
