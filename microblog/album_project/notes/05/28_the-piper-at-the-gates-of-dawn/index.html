mini notes for the piper at the gates of dawn

  • this is the most pink floyd start to an album I can imagine, and this is the pink floyd that was spearheaded by syd… so I guess it makes sense :)
    • the long instrumental break into the almost incoherent second verse / passage is a standout moment for the tracklist imo, as the instrumental is wildly interesting (especially for ’67, which is surprising)
    • I feel it cuts off much too briefly though, a bit of a weird moment
  • especially when it goes right into one sounds like the soundtrack of a spy movie lol
    • the left side right side part, relating someone to a moral miscommunication between the caring, moral part of their brain to the literal devil… it’s literally the most convoluted, yet incredibly apt, devastating way to describe someone :o
    • the track overall is still a bit of a weird mess tbh, I don’t know how to explain it, especially the refrain ^
  • matilda mother is a genuinely intriguing track though, relating heavily to the want of simple childhood back; it’s a worthwhile moment to realize into a track on this record, but it’s not something that personally piqued my interest entirely or at once
  • the springy little harpsichord(?) melody that comes before the guitar section of flaming is a great little moment on the midsection; that was a wild little track, and I think it was the first time I felt confused and immersed in the perfect balance for this record :)
  • pow r toc h has an at-times haunting instrumental, shrouded in a mystique that seems to overtake any individual sound at times; it’s a wonderful piece in the midst of these crazed yet calculated shifts in psychedelic rock :o
  • what a fucking trip on take up thy stethoscope and walk; the track title already is wild, but the content of both the instrumental and lyrics is just so insane that it’s hard to encapsulate in just words alone…
  • great way to get into interstellar overdrive though ngl :o
    • while I hold merit and appreciation for the combinations of sounds that, genuinely at times result in a brilliant hodgepodge of both emaciated and gloated freedom… there are definitely moments that just feel like an indulgence into a trip that went sour
    • it is not bad by any stretch, and instrumentally encapsulates the whole record perfectly without uttering a single word…sometimes it can just stretch itself a little bit thin I think ^
    • might be the best part of the record tho :)
    • ooh that moment with 8:15 to go and the way it explodes is absolutely incredible :D
    • I heard that little moment before the claps… they’ll use that again ;)
  • this is such a mf shift, GNOME MOMENT
    • is this about drugs?
    • this is definitely about drugs
    • yup, this is about drugs :)
  • with the bass and soundscape that comes alongside chapter 24, it may hold a place in my mind as the best moment on the record despite the sprawling vocals
    • it seems to actually hold up very damn well, and the lyrics sit calmly between chaos and confusion, and supreme meaning to even a sober person
  • I have to be honest though… the scarecrow just feels out of place :c
    • that first two verse combination is just alright, ending on a kind note into the instrumental rise— but it seems to just sit and fade, accomplishing only minimal influence with its effort ^
  • yea and I understand the point of bicycle (drugs in a different way, more focused on the instrument of transportation), but god it is just so grating, summed up perfectly with the ending chaos
    • it’s a worthwhile sound, but a lot is just not connecting rn
    • that duck noise is both hilarious and haunting at the same time though, jesus christ :o
