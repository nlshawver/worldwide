mini notes for in rainbows

  • wow, that wild beat in 5/4 alongside the effects that get torn into the mix towards the end of 15 step are absolutely incredible :O
  • god bodysnatchers starts off perfectly, that overbearing, crushing riff :oo
    • that bridge and outro are so mf good, my god... two tracks in and I'm blown away :))
  • nude is absolutely beautiful too, the bass really ties the calmer aesthetic together really well—that extended note from yorke at the end of the second chorus is incredible ^
    • the orchestration too :OO
  • weird fishes/arpeggi is such a weird, wild trip; the meaning is all there but the lyrics are so out there and dipped in random complexities
    • that interlude into yorke's outro is amazing though, on top of the rest of the track :O
  • jesus that bass on all i need :oo
    • god the piano and drums that come in together at the end of this cut are mesmerizing :OOO
    • it's not a lot of happy emotions, or at all, but god these tracks have been beautiful :D
  • i love faust arp and the stream-of-consciousness approach to the lyrics, just blurting out instead if being so calculated as is usual for thom
    • the meaning is also chilling, but it sounds nice; might as well be happy by that and not look into the existential themes :)
  • reckoner is almost haunting in its instrumentation, with thom's vocals being particularly ethereal after that bridge section and the background vocals muttering "in rainbows"
    • the way the instrumental pops back in is lovely :)
  • house of cards is absolutely amazing... the way the instrumental just pops in, the minute intricacies of how it grows, and the constant want of thom's character inhibitions to persuade a woman to run off and get swept up away from these established structures
    • it's incredible, nothing much else to say :O
  • woah, those strings in the middle of jigsaw falling into place are what really got me; the lead-up from the second sprawling verse, and into the final chorus are perfectly chaotic and endearing in its thematic intricacies :))
  • videotape is what got me... that chaotic sampled drum, the cadence and offset rhythm of the piano—it's a fantastic backdrop to some of the most ethereally creeping and show-stopping lyricism with yorke's singing
    • this was an incredible experience to go through again ^^^
