mini notes for circles

  • while I won't be incredibly impressed by mac's voice specifically maybe- I do love the lyrics and the sultry, choked-up articulation to start this LP :)
  • and I wasn't completely into complicated at first, but after that second verse it morphs and peaks with those little synth stings; great stuff :))
    • the final verse and chorus is rly lovely ^
  • god FUCKING DAMN blue world ALWAYS goes hard; I will never not feel absolutely enveloped in those warped, skipping vocals in the background, and mac's lyrics and voice in general are so touching and endearing :DD
  • good news is a beautifully intoned continuation; not nearly as over-powering to the senses, but with that chorus and the way it wonderfully peaks this aesthetic... it's fantastic in the way it carries itself
    • that third verse is absolutely incredible too :OO
  • that intro to i can see is insane :oo
    • god that ethereal chorus is just so lovely :)
    • the way the keys come in after that last chorus is ear candy too; it’s such a peaceful moment, and… it’s just so good :o
  • holy shit… was not expecting the start of everybody at all, that’s captivating as all hell :O
    • I don’t think a lot of the content of the verses are all that profound, but the chorus is nothing if not encapsulating of the pure message of this album ^
  • woods genuinely really comes into its own really well; the verse is a cohesive mess of feelings, and the way the chorus comes off as so simple and pure is absolutely lovely :)
    • might be my favorite, slightly down-tempo track on this record’s midsection so far :)
  • “I made it, but I hate once I build it, I break it” damn… that line is powerful coming at this point in the album, the connection to swimming, and this point in his life…
    • god baro sura’s voice is infectiously touching and beautiful on this track; perfect addition :))
    • absolutely wonderful, kinda playful verse from mac; god this record is such a bittersweet, incredible experience :,)
  • honestly that’s on me is just a simple little almost-ballad; it’s not incredible, but it serves the album well, especially after the combo hits of the past two tracks :)
  • the stuttering vocal doesn’t do as much for me as blue world does, but this is a completely different track; the lyrical specifics are a different moment to be touching down on, and I think for the most part this track does its job nicely ^^
  • surf is another beautiful ballad that marks the albums last stand in a beautiful fashion; all the verses coming from mac feel so subtle and endearingly personal, and the instrumental outro is a beautiful, important moment of rest :))
  • jesus and what an ending- the fact that it ends on such an unstable note is so perfect for this record, and I can’t come away any different than somberly introspective :,)
