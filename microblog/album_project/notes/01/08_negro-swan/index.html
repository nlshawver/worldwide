notes for january 8

Negro Swan by Blood Orange

Orlando

punchy instrumental with some sweet vocal lines right out of the gate :)

kind of a weird synth placement, but the vibe of the track overall has such a good cohesive flow- feels like nodding in and out of a trance

and I like that outro to the track, wraps it up quite well

Saint

nice synth keys with these layered vocals :O

saxophone will always be a good choice ESPECIALLY with that out of no where beat track

I think it looses a bit of its luster soon after, but it’s still a great combination with the rest of the instrumental

that is such a nice interlude though, damn

Spreading all my love for you
You never tell me all that you do
Quiet when it's done, will you ever run?

Take Your Time

I do really like Hynes’s vocals, but I think at times it can be a bit tinny in nature, which I don’t really mesh with as well as I’d like; it is still really damn good though obviously

while the basis of these instrumentals have been these bedroom synths, I absolutely love the extra instrument choices, like the saxophone last track, and the winds this time

yea that ending feels transcendent as ever

Hope (feat. Puff Daddy & Tei Shi)

sort of an abrupt change of pace from the last track, but this stuttering, drunken backing track is so nice of a change-up

Tei Shi does a really good job with the verse, it feels so soulful and like a response to Devonté’s previous few tracks

that little stutter right before the chorus is played out a lot, but it never ceases to compliment almost every track that uses it

I don’t think Puff is saying anything fantastic or out-there, but the combination with the continuing vocals and the improvisation on the piano just makes it such a nice energy on the ending portion of this one

with such a nice chord that fades out the track :D

Jewelry

these talking breaks with instrument improvisations are so nice, it’s like an introspective break every once in a while

smooth as butter chorus… with a break right before a punchy drum beat that genuinely kinda caught me off guard

god that chorus feels so good to hear

wow that is a powerful ending, that’s such a meaningful set of lines that just ring out for such a small amount of time before the track is cut short:

Red and blue lights on my stone
Go back to being unknown
Relax your hair
Tuck your shirt
Put your glasses on
Play your guitar

Family (feat. Janet Mock)

that’s a damn well made interlude, I love it- using the same beat as:

Charcoal Baby

this song just feels so perfectly well done, everything comes together in such a powerful yet smooth-as-silk way in each chorus

and I love that the last chorus is cut in a way that gets right to:

Can you break sometimes?
No one, no one

and my god… the beat change is immaculately done; five out of five track

Vulture Baby

jesus, that is a shocking cut off from the last track, into this jazzy beat break with some nice vocals that go along with this energy

Chewing Gum (feat. A$AP Rocky & Project Pat)

this is a nice change up again, even if the track isn’t all that unique or incredibly interesting at the onset

but even then, everything just sounds so good as a cohesive piece

honestly wasn’t sold on Rocky’s verse at first, but that is

and jesus what an outro, that just feels intentionally off-kilter and uncomfortable

Holy Will (feat. Ian Isaiah)

I wouldn’t understand that shrill intro without these high and soulful lines that come right after, they seem to contrast each other in a good sort of way

with the lack of much instrumentation I can’t help but feel left out- but only to a point, until these harmonized vocals come in and just feel so inviting and exciting

I think this could have been a bit of a drag-on if not for those slight instrumentals and the constant altering of those instruments used

some very nice chords into a well-place off-pace drum beat, the ending works very well for me

Dagenham Dream

honestly I keep getting surprised by Hynes’s voice, it does have a relatively good range of personality in different contexts that can work wonders for these different tracks

Broken teeth and bloody nose but least it snowed
Instantly dry, oh, I cried and cut a line
In my eyebrow acted like the boys in town
Then my teacher told me that this, made her sad
Had to act just like the others to get around
Friday nights alone in Ilford with my board

this is such a good piece of the verse, knowing all of the meaning and hearing what he’s saying feels exactly like something that all of us have dealt with at times- which just works so well to make this feel personable

especially with the outro that has almost exactly this same message- it works so well alongside his unique perspective (to many) of feeling like the ‘Negro Swan’ this record is named for, in comparison to those around him

Nappy Wonder

ooh, some nice chord progressions with perfectly spaced kickdrums to my ears- and these synths just feel so nice with the completely overblown guitar

and such a nice pre-chorus with some smooth wordplay:

I'm riding switch, I can't escape
I bust it up on Ilford Lane
A pleasure flip up to my grade

it feels so perfectly chaotic, it’s pretty and crazy and contrasts perfectly to get right to this last chorus

Runnin’ (feat. Georgia Anne Muldrow)

this is exactly what you’d want to hear in a slump, this type of track just feels pleasant with this type of energy:

Just hold on to your mighty way of being

I don’t even know how to say how this record makes me feel, it feels like a cohesive unit of spoken word and these smooth, soulful verses that work wonders

Out of Your League (feat. Steve Lacy)

this overblown kick-drum bit with these vocals are again a bit of an exciting turn in comparison, and I love it

dude I told you, that stutter gap is immaculate

Minetta Creek

more nice piano lines, with these buttery vocal lines that I love- typical great stuff from this album

Find a means to drive away grief
Choose your sex to find a relief
Nothing is forgiven, black skin and my rhythm
And you know that it's all at a cost

that might have been my favorite line on my second favorite instrumental, damn that’s powerful

Smoke

these are some beautifully articulated vocal lines over this amazing guitar section- this is such a good outro to such a good record
