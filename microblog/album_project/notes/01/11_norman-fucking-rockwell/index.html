notes for january 11

Norman Fucking Rockwell! by Lana Del Rey

Norman fucking Rockwell!

woah, I was not expecting this… the piano under these vocals is pretty stunning

that verse two has a surprising amount of meaning behind, I like this so far ^

and jesus her voice is soaring before it fades into muddiness behind the instrumental

that’s not at all what I was expecting but it delivered, that’s good

Mariners Apartment Complex

a very subtle instrumental, and while I was doubting in the first verse, that pre-chorus caught me again and was surprisingly emotive

and the chorus to follow it up is very good too, I’m just really not a big fan of the instrumental with the instrument choice- even if the actual harmony and melody made by them are good

the ending synths for me kinda pick this up, especially with the dreamy vocals- that is a very nicely composed ending imo

Venice Bitch

wow, this starts really well, and the post-chorus sticks out to me most; while this is not something I’d listen to often, this seems to be one of the best and well-made examples of this genre

ohh I’m really not a fan of those drums… just reminds me of a modern church :(

the rest of the instrumentation is really sweet and mellow, which is the part that makes me the saddest, cause I just really didn’t mesh with that beat change; when it comes in again quietly though it is much better tho

around this crazy guitar solo and the acoustic guitar going nuts with the chords; I love the chaos that turns into really well-fitting melodic pieces

okay the second beat change when it gets the most intense this record has gotten to yet is really well done, I have to say… and that comes down really quickly from that chaos, pretty big tonal shift but it’s executed well

and I guess it’s a compliment that that didn’t feel like nine and a half minutes at all

okay revision on second listen- those drums are not that bad, that was kind of overblown given how it actually fits pretty well (and also this is my third favorite now)

Fuck it I love you

staying this low and mellow I think is the only good way to move away from Venice Bitch, so good start in addition to a good verse and very good pre-chorus/chorus combination :)

okay the cadence on the chorus is really getting to me with how high her voice can get without feeling shrill, this is my favorite track so far ^

wow and I haven’t complimented her songwriting ability as much as I should’ve, that bridge was powerful when it goes right back into the chorus

Doin’ Time

ohhhh okay I know this- oh my god this mf instrumental slaps :O

AHHH THAT BEAT DROP, that was genuinely such a jaw-dropping moment on this record

god damn this thing is so smooth, expressive, and just pretty soulful especially compared to what I thought of the opening of this record

and the beat just keeps getting better, those small details between the bridge and final chorus are stunning even if they’re so subtle

Evil, we've come to tell you that she's evil, most definitely
Evil, ornery, scandalous and evil, most definitely

jesus that kinda got me, that’s a damn good line in a five out of five track; I eat my ‘new favorite’ words from just a few minutes ago

Love song

okay especially with this come down romance track after Doin’ Time gives me a kind of new perspective on this thing, it doesn’t have to be all that exciting and these slow melodies are soothing and it makes the notable moments more momentous

that chorus was really good, and it just ends so smoothly right into the next verse- which is stunning, I might add

god the strings in this last chorus and outro are like a drop-to-your-knees kind of moment- and it’s powerful and moving without needing at all to be bombastic

Cinnamon Girl

oooh that drop into the chorus with that rumbling bass line- and the SYNTH right after that’s just sneaking in is so smooth

and dude… this chorus and post-chorus is beautiful, I’m so glad it comes back to it again and ends on this somber tonality over a stunning instrumental

and while I think I’d normally think that the outro drags on, appreciating it as almost an interlude and enjoying this very well-made instrumental is a gift to my ears

How to disappear

yea her voice is stunning, whatever

this is another really good instrumental; not as special yet as what the past few tracks have been to me but, especially with sleigh bell aesthetic it changes up really well ^

and again, the actual vocals are so stunning without being drawn-out or anything close to too dramatic

idk why so many songs have great outros to me, but that’s another good one

California

I really love the careful desperation on the chorus, it’s genuinely amazing with the instrumentals almost leading this along

and I think that second verse through to pre-chorus and chorus is my favorite patch of this song, it’s such a good peak ^

that was a sort abrupt ending, but it transitions into the next song really well I think:

The Next Best American Record

The greatest

and I just love how extremely nostalgic this track is, it’s hyper-focused and very well-done imo

jesus… that is a great outro with these keys backing up the fade out

Bartender

ooh… the static around her vocals is an interesting choice(?), but that long chorus is a very good pick in my eyes since this track is almost entirely her incredible vocals and this smooth piano melody

I bought me a truck in the middle of the night

Happiness is a butterfly

Hollywood and Vine, Black Rabbit in the alley

my god, best outro by far with her vocality
