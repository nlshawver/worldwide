notes for january 29

Nevermind by Nirvana

Smells Like Teen Spirit

damn this opener, what the hell :O

this track might be played too much, but at least it’s an incredible track; this is so mf good ^^

With the lights out, it's less dangerous
Here we are now, entertain us
I feel stupid and contagious
Here we are now, entertain us
A mulatto, an albino
A mosquito, my libido

every single instrument just does such an incredible job, and Cobain’s vocals fit wonderfully with the attitude of the track; genuine masterpiece of an opener, and such a mood-setter for the record

In Bloom

god that guitar melody right before the verse is such a catchy opener- and THAT BASS LINE :O

We can have some more
Nature is a whore
Bruises on the fruit
Tender age in bloom

what an incredible and brutally meaningful verse right into the aggression of the chorus—

I don’t know how you can have a record brimming with classics with this much of a unique energy, it’s legitimately magnificent

Come as You Are

this is just proving that point- another instantly recognizable track with the warbled, creepily meandering bass; brilliant three opening tracks, my god

I don’t have much else to say about this track that hasn’t already been said about the last two, it’s useless to try and encapsulate energy put forth

Breed

holy shit :O

I’m sorry those bass and guitar lines are just jaw-dropping, I don’t know what to say- AHHH WTF THE SOLO

this thing is impossible to describe; this is as masterful and iconic as Illmatic is for its own genre (and music as a whole)

Lithium

I love the temporary pull-back in terms of energy, but it just delves right back in the dredges with the chorus and its theme

I can’t keep saying everything is amazing and incredible with different words; I think no matter what you know this record is a masterpiece

Polly

the way this track genuinely pulls back now with this acoustic melody is lovely, and it just adds to the wonderful pacing of the record :)

that chorus and its cadence is incredibly well done, it sticks out the most in this progression (in a good way, of course)

Territorial Pissings

jesus what a crushing theme and a brutal instrumental and vocal tonality to go with it; just looking at the meaning behind this track is so moving, and this is in the midst of quite possibly the most moving record of the past half-century

Drain You

honestly this is the only track that feels like a continuation of old themes on the record- but even then the dorky meaning of the song makes it unique and special on its own ^^

Lounge Act

I think the track name perfectly encapsulates the energy of this track- this is the closest thing to a grunge/alt-rock track made on top of an elevator music melody

damn what a perfectly unique and mind-altering ending :O

Stay Sway

I’m glad I only know a few tracks on this record, I think it makes the experience of the back-end of this record so much better

Monkey see, monkey do (I don't know why)
Rather be dead than cool (I don't know why)
Every line ends in rhyme (I don't know why)
Less is more, love is blind (I don't know why)

jesus christ what an expressive, knotty ending; that was incredible as always :O

God is gay

On a Plain

I adore that bridge, that’s such a fantastically well-articulated break in the track- and the comeback to the verse is wonderful :)

this is a perfect, interestingly unique and colorful deep cut on this record ^

god and those ending vocals are lovely and soothing ^^

Something in the Way

what a haunting track, I can’t accurately describe the feeling garnered from this, so you’ll just have to listen for yourself

which is something you should do regardless, this record deserves every single second of its runtime

Endless, Nameless

this is an absolutely brutal and crushing end to the tracklist, and just completely topples the expectations of the ending on Something in the Way

no more words are needed, you know you should listen to this again
