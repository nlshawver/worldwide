notes for january 6

The House Is Burning by Isaiah Rashad

Darkseid

alright, good start yet again for these records I’m reviewing, this slow meandering instrumental is nice

that was a very good verse, I love Isaiah’s voice in this because of how smooth it is with that instrumental ^

interesting stop, and a simplistic transition to

From The Garden (feat. Lil Uzi Vert)

huh… I don’t know how I feel about that staccato inflection…

or that chorus, even though I think the verse ends pretty well

Uzi’s verse is honestly pretty good though, probably the best part of this song in my opinion

yea I’m just really not a fan of this chorus- but the outro is a high point just because of the energy of it and the recording

RIP Young

and that was a very smooth transition, I like that a lot :)

yea this style of song and inflection seems to compliment Isaiah’s voice much more, this is very good

that backing vocal track in the chorus, alongside the very well placed instrumental sounds so damn smooth

Lay Wit Ya (feat. Duke Deuce)

damn that is a slick instrumental behind this style of verse, I love it

I don’t think the intentionally lo-fi lyrical tone is usually good, but it fits this track very well

I think Duke Deuce’s feature is not that bad, and it certainly ends on a very high note, but I can’t help but think that Isaiah keeping this feature-less might have complimented the style a lot

either way, still a banger of course ^^

Claymore (feat. Smino)

ooh, when that verse comes in… that is damn good

this stretch of four songs (including the next, cause I already heard it) is genuinely great :)

Smino fits very very well on this chorus too, just to say; and while the verse I don’t think sticks the landing nearly as well as it started, is still very good in my opinion

Headshots (4r Da Locals)

god this song is so fantastic; that chorus alone just feels so smooth, it’s incredible- but the verses just add to the energy, and fit so well in the mix

I got a crib bigger than Budapest
And the shots ain’t bringin’ my soldier back

that is a damn good double entendre on ‘shots,’ probably best lyrical part of this song, and the record so far

All Herb (feat. Amindi)

I don’t think a lot of these songs are all that profound, especially in terms of lyrics, but the mix of the energy of the tracklist, and the instrumentals being so smooth and well-placed, I can’t help but think that makes up for it

same with this track- it just seems like a personal story that doesn’t necessarily convey directly as well as I would otherwise think, but the lyrics that do land and connect (with me specifically, of course), alongside that beat behind it, it just feels right

but damn that Amindi verse actually fits and meshes so well, I like that a lot :)

Hey Mista

such a smooth chorus, my god

I’ll be honest in saying I saw theneedledrop’s review of this, but I don’t see what he means when all of Isaiah’s parts are outshined by the features, or that he’s boring when he is on the track- I think the tracklist so far sort of disproves that, since the chilled parts feel so intentional, and it meshes way better with me then it did with him I guess

and jesus that second verse on this was surprisingly good, with the change in attitude being so punchy

True Story (feat. Jay Rock & Jay Worthy)

this first verse is the only verse I can understand what he meant though, it’s just a bit washed out- but I think the chorus with Amindi he

but DAMN yea Jay Rock carried this track, I have to admit; I don’t think that’s a bad thing, and it compliments the attitude of this track well, but I see exactly what he means

Wat U Sed (feat. Iamdoechii & Kal Banx)

this is just another smooth track on here, I like the vibe of this too

and I think Iamdoechii does make a statement with her verse, and it does seem clearer than both Isaiah and Kal Banx’s parts on the track, but I like that sort of energy (even if it’s definitely not my favorite track)

it just feels like a nice shift with those playful synths over the beat

and that was a neat outro, I like that :)

Don’t Shoot

damn okay, I don’t know- I seem to really like this Memphis style of these powerful & punchy beats with these slightly washed-out, mellow lyrical flows

it’s simplistic and smooth, and just a style I really like- especially with this track

Chad (feat. YGTUT)

okay I was doubting this track at first but damn, this might be the third favorite right now- the beat is so damn smooth, and I love the lyrics, with verses trading back and forth in the mid-section between Isaiah and YGTUT

and while I think I’d like to see more of YGTUT on this, I think that just compliments the style of the song as a whole, and isn’t a dig on Isaiah at all (the reason I like it is because they’re both there, not one or the other; which I think is one way a feature can work really well)

9-3 Freestyle

not much I feel drawn to in this track, just seems to be a neat interlude

and some of the bars that are in this are more energetic on the last couple tracks, so I think it’s a bit more of an energetic song on purpose

Score (feat. SZA & 6LACK)

okay this might not be one that sticks out to me much, but I do really like the energy of this track, especially this slower part of this first verse:

War scars and more shit
You got some baggage, emotions
Crash and control issues
Lagging and loading
Heart was too open
I put that over peace

I love that piece, especially when SZA’s part is soon after the chorus- I like this :)

and 6LACK’s part was actually pleasantly surprising, I like how he fits into this track- didn’t think it’d work as well as it did

THIB

alright, even though I’m not as much of a fan of the instrumental of this, I do really like the personality of the vocals, and how it pertains to Isaiah’s struggles; I think it’s pretty well made, for sure

and the chorus definitely fits, feels natural almost

that was a harsh beat change, but I’ll let it slide given the themes of this track, and how well

HB2U

it transitions into this track- very nice :)

I think the five song grouping from RIP Young to All Herb is first only against these last two tracks- I really like this exit on the record

it’s not terribly unique compared to the rest of this record’s style, but I don’t care, it sounds good; and I like how clear Isaiah’s vocals are after he talks about his problems with alcoholism- that’s such a good thematic focal point, especially as this record fades out

but of course right when I say that there’s this sad and drawn-out conclusion to the whole album:

I should just pack up my bags and get loaded
She sick and tired of distance, I’m a ghost
This ain’t the time of my life, but I’m still on drugs
Just had a weekend buzz
Have I been cheatin’ myself? I’m implodin’

jesus, and after such a long piece, it’s just over– that is a well made ending in my eyes
