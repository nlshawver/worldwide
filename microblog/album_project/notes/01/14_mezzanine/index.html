notes for january 14

Mezzanine by Massive Attack

Angel

going into this I have no expectations; I've never heard this before, so I'm blind to what this is exactly

I can tell you though that this introduction is incredible, the soundscape that's being formed with that bass line, these echoed vocals, and ooh that beat ^

dude that drop :O

I absolutely love the driving guitar riff and that stuttering drum beat capped off by the snare hit, this is literally just music to rob a bank to

jesus what an opening track, that was insane

Risingson

my god, that's another damn good groove to start this off

these vocal lines are really impressive too, they sound so perfect alongside this instrumental

god those "dream on" lines that fades at the end of 3D's verses are so good :)

Teardrop

that's another really good beat introduction with that harpsichord; this is already so stellar for this type of record- and I was not expecting those vocals :O

I honestly don't know what to say, that's just a really good track in my eyes, the instrumentals on this thing are already stunning

Inertia Creeps

ooh, I was not expecting those open snare hits with the warped and squirming sort of bass line

Can't endure, then you can't inhale clearly
Out of body experience interferes
And dreams of flying, I fit nearly
Surrounds me, though I get lonely slowly

jesus these vocals feel so sinister and punching, I love this; and god that bass line sounds like it's out of a tron movie, I adore that kind of styling

Exchange

that's a nice soothing instrumental introduction– and wow that supple, analog-ish bass line is rly good

a nice little instrumental break, I like that :)

Dissolved Girl

this track doesn't stand out to me a ton as of right now- but after that first chorus and that explosiveness, that does pick it up for me a bit ^

okay I eat my words, the instrumental after that slaps, I love that beat :O

and the chorus in itself is rly good too, idk why I was being so critical about it before lol

this was really good ^

Man Next Door

god that drum style is never gonna get old :)

I'm not blown away by the vocals all that much in this one, but it does fit and towards the end of the verse the energy meshes much better imo

and I do rly like the way that fades out too ^

Black Milk

god just another fantastic bass riff to go along with these dreamy vocals :)

same singer as on Teardrop, Elizabeth Fraser- and while I’ll need to listen to both of them again I do think I like this track a bit more

both are really damn good of course, but still

Mezzanine

ooh I love how faded and echoed this instrumental is; and those low-pitched vocal lines are a great thematic choice too, sounds like a response back and forth :)

damn that’s such an aggressive tone, for a title track on this tail end of the record that’s a surprisingly good shift ^

Group Four

ooh weird, this is a hypnotic type of beat- and the vocals are as expected from this record (but that doesn't make Elizabeth Fraser's vocals any less beautiful)

Ready to sing
My sixth sense peacefully placed on my breath
And listening
Keeps me from my hurt
My ears know that my eyes are closed
Perished thoughts like contraband

that is a very cryptic and lovely chorus, so nice ^

ah and that long break between the fourth verse and Fraser's outro is so good, especially when that outro begins with the driving bass line through the whole thing

(Exchange)

I do kinda like the revisiting of this track with vocals, I just think it being the full song yet again makes this record feel a bit dragged out; I had those thoughts earlier, where the tracks become just a hint too long, and the whole thing sort of gets lost in its own energy

thank god that energy is really damn good

but yea anyways this track is pretty good, I am actually appreciating more his vocals on this, it's charming almost
