notes for january 15

self-titled by Led Zeppelin

Good Times Bad Times

jesus this is one of the best discography introductions ever, the first four bars are so iconic

there's gonna be a lot of compliments for these guitar solos lol

and my god the drums too of course; this track is incredible, I don't know what else to say

Babe I'm Gonna Leave You

hmm I don't think I've heard this before-

wait that instrumental splash before he says baby a ton sounds really good (and familiar)

my god that screaming vocal line was honestly really damn good

wait that guitar riff is so familiar, I had no idea it was in this; I like that a lot tho

that was honestly really good, better than I expected; and that ending was really well done with just Plant's vocals and that subtle guitar line ^

You Shook Me

huh, I didn't really click with this at first but as it went on I think the personality of it carries the track; and that organ piece is shockingly fitting here

I have to be honest with you though, that harmonica part is gross- and while the guitar solo piece is better by a wide margin, I'm really not liking this

the ending is okay tho, the vocals are kinda impressive in all honesty

Dazed and Confused

but thank god I get to listen to this absolute masterpiece now- that introduction with the commanding bass line, the soaring guitars throughout the track, and that buildup to the crescendo is absolutely stunning

god that mf guitar is so perfect, that's some goosebumps

have to compliment how shrill Plant's vocals are, but they're still so shredding and somehow soulful at the same time ^

Your Time Is Gonna Come

oh god I'm not a huge fan of this already

somehow that chorus isn't all that bad to me, I just really don't like the very obvious meshing of country and rock; it just sounds a bit forced and just too much for me

the outro is way better to my ears since it's not tied to that aesthetic

Black Mountain Side

I do like that fading transition, and I think that instrumental is honestly kinda charming- especially given that it's an interlude

Communication Breakdown

alright this is pretty good, but honestly I don't entirely get the appeal; it doesn't have nearly as much of a personality as the first few parts of this record

I will admit that this song is vastly better and more iconic than the more bluesy stuff they made on this record; it feels kinda forced and unnatural to me, I'm just not a huge fan

I Can't Quit You Baby

yea like this- I'll admit the guitar sounds rly damn good here, but the attitude isn't quite there for me

there are a few genuinely really good guitar riffs and it's well made of course; it just does not mesh at all with my ears

How Many More Times

same with the intro of this, like come on

like the midsection is this song is genuinely good, since they're sticking to more of the rock sound, but that intro is just gross given the rest of the track

and okay, the marching along aesthetic of the outro (of the first part) is rly good and a great pick up from the rest of what I didn't really like

yea this is really good, movie soundtrack energy ^

OHHHH when the drums come back in- that's the most out of pocket thing this record has done and I love it, pretty good ending to this weird record
