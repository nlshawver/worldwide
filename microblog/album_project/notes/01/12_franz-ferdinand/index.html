notes for january 12

self-titled by Franz Ferdinand

Jacqueline

what a bombastic, hedonistic introduction to this record :O

and that’s such a shredding, vibrant verse into this second chorus; both of these instrumental breaks are incredible

Tell Her Tonight

what a quick transition into this completely different and slick track, I love the choice of instruments, and just how shockingly well these tracks work in being so colorful (despite the lo-fi mastering)

great starting two tracks, but…

Take Me Out

come on dude— how does this song even exist

you just know from the intro vocals this is gonna be great, and the guitar line is magnificent… but then you get the punchy and groovy break into the first verse that still stuns eighteen years later

also I had no idea: this song released on the twelfth of january 2004- exactly eighteen years ago

I just have to compliment, that while of course the drums and bass are what make most of this beat (outside of the primary guitar melody), those backing guitar lines are masterful

masterpiece of a track :D

The Dark of the Matinée

and surprisingly, the next track somehow lives up to that last one- maybe not in legacy and recognizability, but seriously this track is the best ‘deep cut’ on the record

the backing guitar is yet again a fantastic addition, but seriously: these vocals and bass line are so satisfying to bob your head along to :)

and god that bridge is so playful up until the last few lines, where it feels almost menacing before flashing back into the chorus :O

You will find me and follow me through corridors, refectories and files
You must follow, leave this academic factory

this is the best line so far, feels so natural ^

Auf Achse

that’s a very groovy introduction; the instrumentals are seriously so well-made and generally stand out to me so far

this is just a well-made post-punk sort of track, which of couse means its so infectiously catchy

a very well done bridge and outro to go alone with that too, that was honestly slightly surprising- and in a very good way ^

Cheating on You

ooh, that’s a very fiery introduction, and that guitar riff is infectious :O

I think the production is pretty muddy, but given how intense this song is I don’t even blame it; and that chorus and especially the outro is delivered so intensely

great track honestly, that’s an actual quality deep cut

This Fire

yet another great set-up to these weird, discordant & layered vocals- I think that chorus is the only weak part because this beat is absolutely fire (yes intended)

okay on further examination, this track is insane- such a 2000’s style banger that hits pretty much everything right

that slow reintroduction of all the instruments hits SO well

is this alternative rock playboi carti?

Darts of Pleasure

ooh that is an incredibly stylish, smooth introduction and first verse

You are the latest contender
You are the one to remember
You are the villain who sends a
Line of dark fantastic passion

that just sounds so good coming from Alex Kapranos; and AH that stutter :)

and oh my god that german outro sounds so smooth coming from a scottish band lmao

Michael

another stunning guitar riff, and that bass line is not bad at all ^

this one honestly might be my least favorite so far, but it’s still pretty good- and that outro kinda ties it together for me and it just shined at that point

Come on Home

OOOH that intro is so damn well made, so groovy and I just love the vocal lines:

Although my lover lives in a place that I can't live
I kind of find I like a life this lonely
It rips and pierces me in places I can't see
I love the rip of nerves, the rip that wakes me
So I'm dissatisfied, I love dissatisfied
I love to feel as though it's more than I need

there’s so many stunning tracks on this record that aren’t even close to as popular as their others, and this is one of them for sure ^

jesus that guitar :O

honestly surprised by that outro, I really like how somber is gets compared to the rest of the track

40’

oh my god, that quiet plucking just feels so good, and it grows into this amazing combination; this is such a fantastic track so far

gotta love the reverb on the guitar, that’s such a good fit with the vibe- and great vocals as always :)

Salt scales upon my drying arms
Burn my back beneath the sun
But I am cold beneath the burning rays
Looking down, looking down, down, down again

outside of “oh forty feet remain” that is a great verse to end on

and OH MY GOD this ending instrumental break is absolutely crushing; that sting to end is a perfect end to dare I say the most succinct indie rock album I have heard
