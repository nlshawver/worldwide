notes for january 5

Turn On The Bright Lights by Interpol

Untitled

ooh alright, I like this instrumental :)

I do like this intro where the actual vocal segment takes up so little time, but it’s still integral to the feel/attitude of the song

that fade out is really good too

Obstacle 1

another really good instrumental, with the short guitar riffs– but the vocal lines are way punchier than I was expecting, and in a great kind of way

this is actually really good, I’m not a huge fan anymore of this alternative sound but it’s still composed surprisingly well- and while I think some of the vocal lines could be more powerful, I think the expression in them is more than enough to make them fit perfectly in with the sound

NYC

honestly wasn’t expecting this change in energy so quickly, but I love this first verse and chorus, along with of course its meaning

wow… the instrumental after the second verse and leading into the bridge is done so damn well

this might be my favorite so far, I love this style and expression

PDA

alright another tonal shift :O

I was unsure of this track at first, but honestly after the first chorus and the start of the second verse I think it’s pretty alright

yea still unsure about that chorus, just with his vocal intonation

this guitar instrumental right after seems to fit really well though, I like this a lot; and that is a damn good outro too

Something to say
Something to do
Nothing to say
When there’s nothing to do

Say Hello to the Angels

damn good instrumental lead up ^^

alright I can see why it fits so well, the meaning of this track certainly fits with the feel of it lol

okay okay, this pre-chorus is surprisingly good, and there are some really good instrumentals around here- LIKE THAT BASS

this is currently tied with NYC, I like both of them for completely different reasons

Hands Away

okay then, compelling start—

alright the drawn out chords/strings that play when the drums come in faster are a very good fit, I like this a lot

the song kinda goes nowhere with this tho, but that seems to fit the energy of the song anyways

Obstacle 2

I really like the start of this again, the second verse sold me on this one

honestly kinda like this more than Obstacle 1, that was surprisingly good given the sort of weird energy/theme on this album as a whole

Stella Was a Diver and She Was Always Down

okay it seems like Interpol are really good at giving a similar start or theme to their tracks, but there’s always one thing that keeps them separate from each other- on this, I love the kind of staccato, pausing drum beat at the pre-chorus

I think the chorus is honestly pretty weak at the start, but the ending of it right into the break (before the bridge) is really good in my opinion

I don’t know, maybe the grimy, imperfect aesthetic just isn’t reaching me on this one; I can recognize that this is pretty good, it’s just not meshing with me much at the moment

but even then, there are bits and pieces that stand out a lot to me, like the style of the break- I just can’t get over how I don’t really like the chorus all that much rn

this outro is good though, I genuinely like this part

Roland

this is another really damn good introduction, I love this style

but seriously, I just can’t get as much into the vocals- the chorus is good but the verse just sets me off a bit

and then it just happens again :/

I don’t know man, the ending is alright, I think this is just my weak point on the record

The New

all of these introductions are so good, I just sometimes can’t mesh with the vocal themes and choices all that much I guess

this is a sort of exception though, this sounds really good so far

yes definitely, this pre-chorus is a jam :)

Leif Erikson

okay this was surprisingly good; I seem to like their songs more so when Banks’s voice isn’t so strained, and he’s able to emote more with lower intonation- or maybe I’m spitballing, I don’t really know myself

but this was very good; I think this, NYC, Obstacle 2, and The New are my clear favorites
