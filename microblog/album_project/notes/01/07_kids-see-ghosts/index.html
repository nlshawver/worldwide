notes for january 7

self-titled by KIDS SEE GHOSTS

Feel The Love

Cudi is already incredible, you know it

if I’ll be honest I didn’t know that was Pusha-T on that first verse, but it goes so well with this vibe

and then its just KANYE SCREAMING

I don’t know why this goes hard, but I think it has something to do with the insane instrumental- shit is so

Fire

damn you already know this is so good with the instrumentals, all of them on this record are so mf good

a good verse on Kanye’s part goes into an incredible verse by Cudi, and the OUTRO is amazing

it’s marching forward through Cudi’s amazing vocals, it just feels amazing

short and sweet outro, right into one of the craziest songs on the record:

4th Dimension (feat. Louis Prima)

great sample that fits this like a glove, right into an incredibly punchy and lyrically lovely verse from Kanye, the metaphors and lyrical tricks are so well done in my eyes:

It feels so good, it should cost
Bought a alligator, I ain’t talkin’ Lacoste

She seem to make me always feel like a boss
She said I’m in the wrong hole, I said I’m lost

That’s too new to mention, or fit in a sentence
If I get locked up, I won’t finish the sent—

and that laughter is ridiculous, and the one part that knocks me out of it for a second, but it’s somehow perfect to go right to Cudi’s verse right after- it’s like a huge contrast despite the instrumental

also damn Cudi’s verse ends so well, especially with that Ric Flair line lol

thank you Shirly Ann Lee :)

Freeee (Ghost Town Pt.2)

JESUS CHRIST THIS SONG

this is the best introduction to a song I have ever heard

I don’t even care that the ‘FREEEEEE’ might seem kinda dumb, it just works for me, I love it in this context

and the harmonization towards the end of the verse and the refrain, it feels transcendent

I love you Cudi

dude you can’t tell me this song doesn’t just feel so powerful and that the synths don’t just do wonders with the drum line and vocals

Reborn

this was a classic right when it came out; you know it was right when Cudi started humming

the whole song is about bringing up, discussing, and growing from your past mistakes and issues- I just think it’s impossible not to feel moved by this

if you don’t get something from this, pick yourself up man… come on

goosebumps at the end of Kanye’s verse into the chorus again, seriously

I can’t really say much else; each track is just a masterpiece, and this is the best mellow, introspective, and emotional song on the whole record

okay but just want to point out the fantastic bass right into the bridge

beautiful track, five out of five easily

Kids See Ghosts

this isn’t even a weak track coming out of Reborn, Cudi’s part is so soulful, I don’t think I can say anything more about how good this is

more incredible sounding basslines, right before a very stand-out verse from Kanye

DAMN the ending of Kanye’s verse is so smooth, I love the final lines:

I won’t relish on the fact I’m vibin’ on the future
Ultralight buildin’ in the buildin’ by Miuccia
Herzog and de Meuron in an office out in Basel
No not Miami, Switzerland

and wow, I just love Yasiin Bey’s whole part on this record, he’s amazing on this just as well as Kanye and Cudi

Cudi Montage

I didn’t think I’d love Cudi rapping as much as I did, this first verse might be the best verse I’ve heard from him since his debut

dude this is the least-fitting verse in the whole record (imo) but it’s still so damn good, I like it so much as well

and this whole outro sounds so powerful, no matter whether you personally relate to the ‘Lord shine your light on me, save me, please’ bars

and just as it begins, it is over in just 24 minutes… this album doesn’t overstate its welcome for a single second
