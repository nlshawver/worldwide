notes for january 26

Illmatic by Nas

The Genesis

True indeed, knamsayin'?

this is such a thematically interesting introduction, and gives a peek into this conversational style, right before…

N.Y. State of Mind

this track is an absolute treat, and a bonafide masterpiece

I never sleep, 'cause sleep is the cousin of death
Beyond the walls of intelligence, life is defined

iconic :O

Thinkin' of cash flow, Buddha and shelter
Whenever frustrated, I'ma hijack Delta

the depth of just those two lines alone is insane, please just look through this entire genius page; it is absolutely worth it

I got so many rhymes, I don't think I'm too sane
Life is parallel to Hell, but I must maintain

I can’t really say much more, this is a lyrical master class, and such a guttural introduction into this era and scene of hip hop

Life’s a Bitch (feat. AZ)

Keepin' this Schweppervescent street ghetto essence inside us
'Cause it provides us with the proper insight to guide us

I need to stop putting in lyrics, but so so many of these bars are both layered with insane attention to detail, and are just drenched in a swagger and swiftness that’s so incredibly infectious

I could put Nas’s whole verse here if I had to put all lyrics that impressed me, jesus christ :O

okay now is the time I actually talk about the instrumental, because honestly the minimalism of it is exactly what compliments the lyricists- and my god that muted trumpet done by Nas’s father Olu Dara is a wonderful sendoff :)

The World is Yours

I think of the two real tracks we’ve seen so far, this has the most technically impressive and lovely instrumental, it feels so funky and so pleasantly classical in its styling- thank you Pete Rock

okay back to Nas:

Born in correction, all the wrong shit I did, he'll lead in right direction
How you livin'? Large or broke on charge cards and mediocre?
You flippin' coke or playin' spit, spades, and strip poker?

and of course:

Born alone, die alone, no crew to keep my crown or throne

the knowledge and artistry this man has is wonderfully shocking :D

Halftime

thank u large professor

but you have to adore that boom bap instrumental, the deep bass line and drums under it—with horns at certain interludes—it’s amazing

But chill, pass to Andre, and let's slay
I bag bitches up at John Jay and hit a matinée

good idea lol

just overall a great track, I love the style changes this record brings in the instrumentals

Memory Lane (Sittin’ in da Park)

case in point with DJ Premier helping on this; those rhodes-esque keys give such an interesting aesthetic to the track, and the record scratching with the sampled chorus is just a testament to this old, skillful style :)

My pen taps the paper, then my brain's blank
I see dark streets, hustlin' brothers who keep the same rank
Pumpin' for somethin', some'll prosper, some fail

just want to point out one of many inter-bar, inter-verse, mid line rhymes with “hustlin’ brothers” and “some’ll prosper”

this record is insane

One Love

god that entire first verse is another treat in itself, I love it :)

Sometimes I sit back with a Buddha sack
Mind's in another world, thinkin'
"How can we exist through the facts?"
Written in school text books, bibles, et cetera
Fuck a school lecture, the lies get me vexed-er

it was always on the mind; that’s such a transcendent start to the third verse :O

I just want to compliment the instrumental, for some reason that music box sort of melody as the main instrument is surprisingly endearing

One Time 4 Your Mind

this was the weakest instrumental for me until I heard that bass line, this is up there with the rest of them; it’s so damn good

I try to stay mellow, rock, well, a cappella rhymes'll
Make me richer than a slipper made Cinderella, fella

this line was both playful and meaningless, but just so well-articulated and it fit so well into the context of this track; incredible :D

Represent

another earworm instrumental, such a great, powerful energy with a simplistic, creepily weird aesthetic

I drink a little vodka, spark a L and hold a Glock for
The fronters, wannabe ill n––s and spot runners
Thinking it can't happen 'til I trap 'em and clap 'em
And leave 'em done, won't even run about gods
I don't believe in none of that shit, your facts are backwards

ya know I think this guy is good at rhyming and constructing lyrical poetry

It Ain’t Hard to Tell

DUDE COME ON- what the absolute hell is this instrumental, this is INSANE

okay that entire first verse is stunning, but what a sendoff:

I drink Moët with Medusa, give her shotguns in Hell
From the spliff that I lift and inhale; it ain't hard to tell

actually, thinking about it, all of these verses are stunning :O

I don’t care, I just don’t care what anyone thinks- this is the best song of the 90’s; it’s such a microcosm of all of what New York, African-American/black, hip hop, and come-up sort of culture was, and it’s so effortlessly clean yet brutally honest and crushing

what a mf treat, my god
