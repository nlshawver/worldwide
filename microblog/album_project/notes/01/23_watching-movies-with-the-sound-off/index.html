notes for january 23

Watching Movies with the Sound Off by Mac Miller

The Star Room

I’m not sure how I feel about this as an introduction; I think the beat was almost trailing along, but Mac’s lines were the only personality I felt in the whole thing

it would’ve felt sterile without his presence, and even then it just felt casual

Avian

felt similarly about this song, but the second verse had much more of a pull on me

I’m still not really feeling this project at all yet; the chorus felt a bit off, but I’m obviously holding out

I’m Not Real (feat. Earl Sweatshirt)

okay this felt and sounded much better, I really liked the ending of Mac’s first verse right into Earl’s verse; that’s nice :)

god I absolutely loved that style of instrumentation and cadence with the verses, that was more of what I’m expecting with this sort of project

S.D.S.

and that’s a slick transition; this sort of chaos in his vocality is a really good shift after I’m Not Real ^^

kind of a crazed interlude, but the transitions of this thing so far are really satisfying :)

Bird Call

hahaha this song is completely ridiculous, and I love it for that so much ^

okay nevermind that got weird wth; that beat still nice though

Matches (feat. Ab-Soul)

oh my god, Mac’s first verse is so smooth, those were his best lines on the record so far :O

Could've been smart, could've been a doctor
Life full of drama, little soap opera
Fallin' in love, lucky that I caught ya
Almost got her knocked up, would've been a father
All about energy, make good memories
I'm the fuckin' Dalai Lama

that was a bit of a slam, I like that a lot :)

I Am Who Am (Killin’ Time) [feat. Niki Randa]

I really like the beat on this and the atmosphere of it, this is great so far :O

Forfeitin' the war, he lays his sword down and walks away
Grabs a 40 from the corner store, begins to contemplate
Dealin' with death like he work in the morgue, absorbin' souls forgotten
He lost his way, starin' down that barrel, thinkin', "Not today"

damn; with the beat that comes during and after this, that’s surprisingly well done ^^

god and I love the soundscape being made here, this is my favorite so far :)

Objects in the Mirror

this is a really smooth, unique sort of beat; I’m liking this too rn ^^

god and that chorus is so nice (even with the completely ridiculous sample going back and forth)

these last two tracks are the best for me so far, it’s peaking rn for me :)

Red Dot Music (feat. Action Bronson & Loaded Lux)

god that end to the first bar is so stupid lol

okay the ending was much better- just taken out by that chorus even if it ends well with that ending line

ooh and that outro was shocking and really well made; I like that as a bit of an inter-song transition/interlude :O

Gees (feat. ScHoolboy Q)

damn this beat with that bass ^^

I love the little lines mixed into the verses, it’s such a weird, incredibly boastful energy

and I like it more than I expected :)

god that schoolboy verse was great too, I just hope this record doesn’t overstate its welcome with how long this lasts

Watching Movies

okay this is a really good sign; it’s interesting but very satisfying to hear Mac’s innocent-sounding vocality over top of this absolutely hard, powerful beat behind him

yea for sure; this is my second favorite rn, that went so well, I love that :)

Suplexes Inside of Complexes and Duplexes (feat. Jay Electronica)

Do as a saint do, turn painful to graceful
The devil on my trails, I'm tryna find the Holy Grail
*Coughs* Right there

that’s genuinely the best “clever” bar that he’s had this record, and I love it over this creeping sort of beat :)

god and Jay Electronica’s verse is such a good compliment to this track, this album’s peak for me has been since I Am Who Am to here ^^

REMember

I think by the halfway point this track gets into its own and becomes a really good introspective break in this record, but I was just not meshing with it at the onset

yea the layered vocals compliment this a lot when you get into it ^

wow and honestly that guitar line fits shockingly well, I think that’s a really good send off for this track

Someone Like You

okay yea this record is not falling off at all so far; it keeps riding the line between too boastful and bombastic, and introspective and light in tone

I really like this album so far, I’m just still put off from the start unfortunately :/

Aquarium

Can you feel it?
No, no, don't—don't answer that, that was a rhetorical question

haha I love that introduction to this track

honestly a lot of these songs after I Am Who Am are just perfectly normal with just pretty good vocality, which is probably why I like them

it doesn’t feel transcendent or shockingly incredible, but it’s still catchy and the lines are well-made for sure ^^

Youforia

okay that was a really good ending for this record, Mac’s singing voice fits so nicely with the entire aesthetic of this song :)
