notes for january 19

self-titled by The 1975

The 1975

I’ve only listened to a few songs off this project, but I always liked the introduction aesthetic to this song, and how well it transitions the album into the next song

can’t say much else here, the vocal harmony is nice, and it’s a pretty good intro imo

The City

damn aggressive drum introduction, and those are some powerful synths :O

this song is so amazingly off-kilter and punchy, I love this track even if it’s just a typical indie pop ballad

Your brother is just sat there, you said that you felt snide
You hope that, that the boy will be alright

that line with Healy’s inflection and accent does sound really damn smooth; the track does feel very overblown, but it kinda fits the narrative in its method- very solid track :)

M.O.N.E.Y.

that very chaotically smooth synth at the onset is pretty nice, and that little splash before the chorus is really damn good :O

just a very silky smooth track, nothing incredible but a very good sound- and the verses are an interesting mesh of almost a spoken word story and the typical cadence of Healy’s voice, which I think is actually pretty endearing

not a huge fan of the final outro lyrics, but that’s a weird, interesting instrumentation at the outset that’s pretty nice ^

Chocolate

my god this mf song

the guitars on this song always stuck out to me, the subtle, gentle plucking is so well done in the verses :)

it’s just the closest thing to an incredibly notable indie pop ballad on this album, and I love it for that so much- the vocal lines are catchy, the overall feel of the song is overpowering, but in a pretty good way

I love this track ^^

Sex

oooh that driving guitar- the vocal lines are pretty good here, but the song just feels so grossly bombastic

which, thinking about it, is entirely the point lol

I don’t know, this song is no doubt incredibly catchy and lovely to listen to at times, but it’s definitely not the high point of the album imo

Talk!

god the bass line definitely carries this song- and while I do really like the concept of being way more experimental with the instrumentation, I don’t think it really meshes well with the vocals, especially at the chorus

ooh yea exactly, verse 4 is really good and fits well, and I think the last chorus is the best by far given the overlapping vocals

An Encounter

nice little instrumental, and a great way to divide and prelude tracks on this record

Heart Out

not the biggest fan of this very marching kind of beat- and the vocal lines feel pretty sparse

that chorus is done pretty well, but it seems to be the only interesting part of the song so far- bruh that second verse lol

okay this kinda grew on me, and it’s definitely not bad by any stretch of the imagination, but it’s just a typical song on the album; that bridge is pretty alright, but the thematic choices make it feel so much better going into the last chorus

Settle Down

ooh, great little smooth transition :)

okay damn this is really mf catchy; was not expecting this :O

I think this might be so far the most perfect combination of the instrumentation they brought to the table, along with Healy’s vocality- they’re much less grating and aggressive, but I think that compliments the style the most

sleeper track on this album for sure ^

Robbers

I do really like the fact that since Heart Out, the soundscape really hasn’t ended bc of those transitions, very nice

I feel like this is probably the weakest of these past three, it doesn’t bring anything additional to the table, and it’s not outstanding to my ears

the drawn out vocal lines are nice at the onset but just sort of fall on deaf ears by the end imo

I appreciate the story that’s being told, and I think it is pretty good for a song, but I just think the instrumentation is too boring to fit with such a heavy, gutting theme

Girls

especially when the album transitions right into this, what the hell?

I’m sorry, the cohesion of this record just got really weird, even if (and especially because) this track is an absolute banger- like the entire plot of the past song just means nothing to me anymore because of this disco-esque, celebrating track

okay anyways, this song absolutely slaps :)

the stuttering 80’s guitars fit amazingly well, and when Healy does a vocal inflection to fit a more exciting song it actually does sound really pleasing ^

12

that’s a nice little somber instrumental, I think that’s really nice given the comedown required after Girls

She Way Out

hmm I do really like the grossly smooth aesthetic of this track, but again I just don’t think this meshes quite well with the previous track- it’s much better than Robbers to Girls though, and this track is very catchy ^^

just a nice deep cut on this record, feels like a safe, car ride song (and in a good way, it feels like what Settle Down was to me: the band’s excited aesthetic without being bombastic, which is amazing to my ears)

Menswear

this is a good cooldown though, fits so much better; and I do actually really like the soundscape developing on this track

I think the bass and snapping/clapping fit the least though- it’s nice but to me it just overpowers the whole thing

it’s fine tho, the warping into Healy’s short verse is really damn good :)

Free bar, that's the point
Spilling Amaretto 'cause of previous joints
I'm sitting with a girl, fortunate placing
Preceding railing racket off a porcelain basin

like seriously, that’s the best set of vocal lines in the whole record imo, it’s so slick ^^

it’s a really fitting story behind it too, being just a day at a wedding; second best track on the album imo

Pressure

woah okay, another sort of aesthetic I wasn’t expecting, that’s nice :O

god this is so instantly catchy what the hell; that little instrumental break into the bridge + chorus was wonderful :)

good deep cut overall ^

Is There Somebody Who Can Watch You?

I don’t think this song fits the aesthetic of the whole album, but I don’t care, this is a nice little ending, and a surprisingly good story and vocality on the track as a whole

this fits really well as a closer
