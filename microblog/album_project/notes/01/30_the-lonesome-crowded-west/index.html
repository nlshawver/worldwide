notes for january 30

The Lonesome Crowded West by Modest Mouse

Teeth Like God’s Shoeshine

wow okay, I was not expecting that introduction to this record—

yea that first chorus and verse is an absolute trip, and then it tones itself down into the midpoint of the song

I’m so taken aback from this tbh, it’s so much different than what I was expecting- WOAH that chorus/bridge transition and the guitar work is absolutely stunning :O

yea that breakdown in the final minute is unabashedly crushing, I love this opener

Heart Cooks Brain

and somehow it gets better- I love the simple, stuttering drum beat and the reverse record scratching in between all of the complex, intertwined vocal lines

all of the instrumentation works together so well, and I’m surprised by how much I like Brock’s raw and incongruent vocal lines

Convenient Parking

god it’s like this song is meant for me- I adore the breakdown into the chorus all about suburban sprawl and the razing of all nature for the sake of ‘convenience’

Soon the chain reaction started in the parking lot
Waiting to bleed onto the big streets
That bleed out onto the highways
And off to others cities built to store and sell these rocks
Well, aren't you feeling real dirty sitting in the parking lot?

I don’t think this track is all that standout any longer necessarily, but given this came our over twenty years ago, this holds a surprisingly significant place in my brain now :)

Lounge (Closing Time)

oh my god I adore this instrumental, it feels so special and like such a groove- and while the layered vocals are somewhat grating, it fits so well to my ears :O

yea just even more of a great transition to the midpoint and instrumental break of this song, with that cadence of the drum section- this is fantastic :D

I like that lyrical callback too ^^

Jesus Christ Was an Only Child

what is this

okay how is that violin actually such a groove-

I do like the meaning behind this track, but it’s hard for me to feel connected to the track given its seemingly random placement in terms of theme and styling

Doin’ the Cockroach

yea we’re right back into the normal aesthetic of the record (which I’m not complaining about, I just don’t know)

oh my god those vocals are so biting, that’s genuinely so damn good with this chaotic, driving instrumental :O

Cowboy Dan

I have no idea why or how, but that first verse genuinely shocked me, I’m not sure why this is connecting so m- oh it’s because I’m from the midwest, that’s right

Standing in the tall grass
Thinking nothing
You know we need oxygen to breathe
Oxygen to breathe

damn, and this subtle breakdown to these more painfully somber vocals are absolutely wonderful

this might be my favorite track so far, I’m not really sure

Trailer Trash

this is surprisingly introspective, and I love the harmonic guitar playing- this genuinely a great transition from the last track, and I can understand why this is one of the most popular tracks of this record

jesus the instrumental breakdowns are so lovingly crafted and incredibly well-done ^^

Out of Gas

I don’t really have much to comment on this one, it’s a very minimal, lovely interlude sort of track :)

I keep finding more places and more tracks to appreciate Brock’s vocals, and this is another one of them

Long Distance Drunk

I really like the (excuse me) drunk sounding combination of instrumentation over these very mellow vocals- it’s a very suiting aesthetic, and I absolutely love it :)

Shit Luck

and we’re right back into these screeching, painful guitar wails, holy hell :O

This plane is definitely crashing
This boat is obviously sinking
This building's totally burning down

I don’t know why but screaming these lines is both incredibly funny and so dark at the same time

the combination is just shocking, and I love it ^^

Truckers Atlas

god that instrumental—especially the drum beat—is wonderfully infectious :)

I'm going to Colorado to unload my head
I'm going to New York City and that's in New York, friends
I'm going up to Alaska, I'm gonna get off scot-fucking-free
And we all did

I love that opener, the whole energy of this track is such a great moment for me so far ^^

and while I think for sure this outro instrumental drags on, I do really like that slow build up with increasing levels of chaos and punchiness in especially the drums- and then it just fades out :O

Polar Opposites

jesus that chorus… absolutely heart-wrenching, and it’s done in such a visceral, real method of articulation

I'm tryin', I'm tryin' to
Drink away the part of the day that I cannot sleep away

okay this is my second favorite track, the minimalism and sheer emotion are really well done to my ears (and brain)

Bankrupt on Selling

this is another heartfelt track, like the rest of the record in all honesty

seriously this album just encapsulates the inescapability of the modern world, especially the hell that is american suburbia; the message behind this album is very, very well made

Styrofoam Boots/It’s All Nice On Ice, Alright

okay I wasn’t sold on this track for a little while, but that backing track once that insane drum beat comes in is legitimately outstanding, I love that sharp energy change, and the intensity on the drums being at the forefront of the track

especially when it keeps increasing in tempo up until it starts fading and then- done :O

wonderful end to a fantastic record :)
