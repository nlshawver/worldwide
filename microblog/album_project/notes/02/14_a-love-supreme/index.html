notes for february 14

A Love Supreme by John Coltrane

Pt. I – Acknowledgement

I’m not sure how I’ll go about reviewing this much compared to the other records I’ve listened to, since I’m not nearly as well-versed in the art of jazz than other genres or music as a whole

but oh my god, if the start of this track doesn’t feel incredibly refined, specifically natured, and wonderfully wide and diverse in its styling and soundscape :O

the saxophone is a certified staple and perfectly intoned, but that drum and bass behind this all is beautiful too :)

I feel like I heard the lick in there somewhere, but I will bar my uneducated ears from taking over my brain ^

god, I don’t know what it’s properly called, but the strained, buzzing sax right before the four minute mark is amazing; and the little mini-melodies and wonderful long tones that come afterwards are such a great touch :D

woah, and there’s a moment at around the 5:30 mark where everything sort of shifts towards the center of my headphones… and I guess it’s fitting that right after everything feels more connected, that the record title is sauntered in by these layered vocals—even if it’s more-so a proclamation

what a wonderful start, I adore jazz music exactly for experiences like this

Pt. II – Resolution

I will always be a fan of upright bass, it has such an interesting, dark, and intriguing complexion to its sound; and the way the piano, sax, and drums explode in after the bass starts it off genuinely shook me a bit :O

you feel so refined and in a mysterious yet perfectly home-centered environment when listening to something like this… I guess it helps that the lead mastermind behind this record is insanely gifted; I would have to assume that helps just a bit ^

and the piano solo (or break, whichever fits better) is a great shift away from the saxophone center this record started off on

not saying I’m not excited for when the sax comes back in though, because of course I’m not :)

and it feels like it slowly walked down and up the stairs about 4 3/4 minutes in, I liked that little sub-section (and the little squeals from it that come not too far after); that’s a great ending as well, as it ever-so-slightly trails out

Pt. III – Pursuance

I do love how all the tracks so far have started with the focal point being one specific instrument in the band: first the sax, then the bass, now the drums—with an incredible little solo if I must say, it’s a nice balance of absolute confusion and chaos, mixed in with incredible attention to detail and skeletal use of rhythm cohesion to keep it all together

it’s nice too when everything comes together after a little more than a minute of being solely the drum-set… but I love that Coltrane and his sax only stays for a bit, and then sounds as if he turns around and walks out the room to grab a whiskey sour or manhattan, just to let the slightly quiet but incredibly detailed sound of the piano shine for a while :)

then he comes back and slams out an incredible solo over the bombastic drum-set, with both of them just going insane :O

ahh, I love the indulgence in that basic but satisfying little micro-melody at around the seven minute mark, especially as a way to close out Coltrane’s incredibly long solo, and give way to the drums to have an explosive finish and leave the bass to fiddle around and provide a nice little respite :)

I didn’t think the bass would get the whole rest of the track to itself, but it has plenty of worthwhile staying power- I love the contrast from the rest of the track, and it just feels so supple and lovely hearing another great musician play around in such an artistic manner

Pt. IV – Psalm

this introduction the first time the timpani’s here just feels like you’re in a foggy european city, it’s so damn well-fit and perfect in its styling ^

I do love the fact that the Coltrane’s sax gets most of the attention as a walk-off for the rest of this record, but I think the fact that the timpani is here, along with the stuttering piano that weaves itself in between the saxophone is what really makes this track

I will say though that the saxophone is the core aspect here though, and it has such an incredible ere of flourishing allure to it all throughout the track

and the way the whole soundscape rises gently into a miniature explosion to cap off the end is so lovely; what an experience… :O
