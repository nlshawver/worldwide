The College Dropout by Kanye West

Intro

what the hell lol

We Don’t Care

anyways- jesus this instrumental is so damn powerful and punchy

I don’t know ye sounds so unique and modern on a record almost twenty years old, but this is a great intro track already :O

I feel like a bit of the luster is nostalgic for a time I never fully experienced, but regardless- that was really damn good :)

Graduation Day

bruh I cannot take this voice seriously

okay okay, John Legend is a good touch and ending to this short little interlude- that’s nice ^

All Falls Down (feat. Syleena Johnson)

another nice, unique instrumental with the acoustic guitar and more thoroughly mixed bass- I love the simplicity ^

I want to act ballerific like it's all terrific
I got a couple past-due bills, I won't get specific
I got a problem with spendin' before I get it
We all self-conscious, I'm just the first to admit it

damn what a great ending to that third verse; I love the conscious side of this track and Syleena’s vocals throughout the whole track :)

I’ll Fly Away

I think this start to the record feels a bit back and forth, but I do kinda like the inclusion of these small interludes in-between the primary tracks

it’s a nice energy, and it meshes really well all things considered

Spaceship (feat. GLC & Consequence)

holy- that deep bass is head-shaking :D

I love the external, transcendent feel of this track thorough its backing vocals and instrumental

and I liked ye’s part, but it feels so good to hear GLC on this track for the second verse ^

In the mall 'til 12 when my schedule had said 9
Putting them pants on shelves
Waiting patiently; I ask myself

and after the rhyme scheme before, this section feels incredible in line with the beat; same sort of feeling with the Consequence verse, it’s a nice change of pace, and both verses match the aesthetic of this track :)

Jesus Walks

alright alright, this is the first track I know separately of the LP; I do like a good amount of this, but I think sometimes the sample and instrumental can feel overbearing and claustrophobic

but in general–especially with the verses in between–it’s an interesting and overall good track ^

okay it is very catchy, it’s really good :)

Never Let Me Down (feat. Jay-Z & J. Ivy)

honestly I felt like Jay’s verse is a bit underwhelming and under-developed- but seriously Kanye’s part on this is amazing :O

I think especially after the themes of the last track and the slightly mellowed intonation present here, it’s a really great follow-up and

I'm not a miracle, I'm a heaven-sent instrument
My rhythmatic regiment navigates melodic notes
For your soul and your mental

oh my god J. Ivy is fantastic on this too; that set of bars and the ending are lovely to hear, and matches up with this track in such a touching, intriguing way

okay Jay’s part at the end is pretty good, and lyrically more skilled imo, I don’t know why he feels so tinny over top of the track though- but seriously: great overall track, and the second best part of this half of the LP

Get Em High (feat. Talib Kweli & Common)

oh my god- nevermind about saying the second best, Never Let Me Down is third best now

it’s so braggadocious and slick that you can’t help but be amazed by it; the instrumental adds a great layering to these multi-line rhymes from both Talib & Common—which are an incredible duo to have as features on this track

just a fantastic track to mark the middle of the record, I love it :)

Workout Plan

$3.99? damn…

The New Workout Plan

HOLY SHI- dude this beat is so crushing, it goes so HARD

ye’s flow over top of all this is so infectious and just so completely expected from Kanye lol

this whole track is so stunning, I don’t even get how this was made- this isn’t my favorite song in how well I think it does artistically, but damn if it isn’t the catchiest :D

and the second part after the interlude is really good with that talk-box and how the theme continues through— and I hear the sample ><

Slow Jamz (feat. Twista & Jamie Foxx)

why does she have to drink water if she’s thirsty?

oooh this is funky; I love the introduction and the mellowed energy overall :)

BRUH the Michael Jackson line wth

In the Chi and I be sippin' Hennessy, play some R&B
While I smoke a b, you can preferably find that I'm a G

jesus christ, what a start for Twista; that was genuinely a jaw-dropping set of bars :O

AND IT KEEPS GOING— I love this track, and it’s such a good start for the second half of this record, especially with how it fits into the progression of the record; the chorus is so damn well-made, the chilled break especially after the Twista verse is lovely ^

thank u mr foxx :)

Breathe In Breathe Out (feat. Ludacris)

okay I don’t know how I feel about this start from Luda, but I’ll let it ride

Always said if I rapped, I'd say somethin' significant
But now, I'm rapping 'bout money, hoes, and rims again

god… that swagger on that line is so damn good :O

okay the Luda feature fits a bit better now, that’s pretty good in terms of the rest of this cut honestly ^

ye with the PHD— the amount of ridiculous, playfully crude bars on this record is somehow charming, even if you end up laughing for a quarter of the track

and that big break where it’s just ye acapella is a great touch, I like that a lot; great track :)

School Spirit (skit 1)

ooh okay, especially with the sample and beat behind ye’s vocals, this aesthetic after Through the Wire is a fitting, lovely continuation

Last Call

okay all of these beats are lovely, this is such a great start to this incredibly long track ^

haha I love the start of his second verse:

Now was Kanye the most overlooked? Yes sir

kanye likes ikea :O
