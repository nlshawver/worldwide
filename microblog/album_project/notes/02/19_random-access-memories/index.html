notes for february 19

Random Access Memories by Daft Punk

Give Life Back to Music

honestly I don’t think there’s much to say here- this record feels like something of what Silk Sonic is, where it’s a modern interpretation and love letter to the peak genres and stylings of 70’s and 80’s music

and this is a really good opener to set that stage :)

The Game of Love

the typical daft punk vocals really make this feel unique and special though, I love the aesthetic :)

god I love this track- the subtle and expressive drum beat, the little vocal harmonizing and lovely bass line behind it all… absolutely wonderful stuff :D

definitely the best track of this introduction to the record ^

Giorgio by Moroder

I love that extended vocal line, it feels like a very personable, expressive way to earn a bit of a connection to this track; and the instrumental that follows it is a nice mix of drum and bass work, and that stunning, warbling synth :D

great little rhodes solo :)

damn that drop, so hungry with the high strings and drum combo :O

that was a great little journey through so many sounds and little improvisations, I loved that ^

Within

aww I thought was gonna be the sickest transition ever... the piano's still really nice after that tho

and yet again the vocals (along with some rly nice bass and drum sections that feel kinda chunky) make this such a great downtempo sort of track

lovely little pause from last track's action :)

Instant Crush (feat. Julian Casablancas)

okay I was just kinda grooving to this at first, with yet again some great drums and bass... but damn that chorus is SO damn good :D

the vocals just feel so wavey and pleasant; it's so good with all these synth leads :)

definitely my favorite track on this LP so far ^

Lose Yourself to Dance (feat. Pharrell Williams)

jesus that is such a heavy, stunning guitar riff draped over this instrumental :O

and pharrell was a perfect choice for this record, his voice is unsurprisingly fitting with this style

this just feels like a retro revival even by daft punk standards, and it works so damn well :)

Touch (feat. Paul Williams)

damn what an introduction- that creeping sort of soundscape is a great choice into this way more rock-ish sound in a way

OH SHIT that piano; this just feels decadent, and a little out of left field... but it's such a good change up imo :)

that was overall way better than I expected, and the final, cut-off crescendo was so damn well-made :O

Get Lucky (feat. Pharrell Williams)

I mean... what can I say about this track that anyone doesn't already know

this is such a staple of the time; the nostalgia and merit of this track I think match due to how good I think this song actually is :D

it just slaps so damn hard (:

Beyond

what is this, super smash bros brawl?

okay but honestly though, this develops into a pleasantly mellow instrumental, with those lovely vocoded(?) vocal lines :)

the feel of the latter half of this record is wonderful, and this is a great continuation of it, especially after last track's energy ^

Motherboard

woah that acoustic guitar is a surprisingly fitting addition, especially with the extra strings which I love too :O

da clarinet ^^

EHHH while I'm still grossed out by how liquid and viscous that sounds, the little beat break, pause, and touching synth leads that introduce it again are lovely :)

Fragments of Time (feat. Todd Edwards)

damn what a groove... this record really is a neatly packaged love letter, it's so well crafted and emotive :)

plus the bass is incredible, so I can't not like it this much

THE VOCALS AND BEAT TOGETHER :D

what a great track, and it leads right into...

Doin’ It Right (feat. Panda Bear)

this is the most true-to-discovery track on this thing, and it slaps SO HARD with that low bass note

it legitimately shakes my soul with that hit; it's such a fantastic, lovable cut of this LP :D

and Panda Bear's vocals and lyrics in general are a really good fit for this aesthetic, they mesh perfectly to my ears :)

Contact

holy shit, what a fantastic vocal sample ^^

these ethereal chord changes are wonderful, I don't even know what to say... but ngl was not expecting the drums to be that uptempo and energetic :O

oh my god, what a mf crescendo even though it's still going(...?) the washed out chaos of the synths is almost haunting ^

and that's all we're left with at the end... that sounded completely alien and insanely charged with energetic wonder, what the hell :O
