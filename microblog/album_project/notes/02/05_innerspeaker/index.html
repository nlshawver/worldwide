notes for february 5

InnerSpeaker by Tame Impala

It Is Not Meant To Be

damn okay, that simple guitar line over this instrumental is a really nice start, I like this so far :)

But, in all honesty, I don't have a hope in hell
I'm happy just to watch her move

ooh I really like that chorus and the echoing filter over Parker’s voice, it really makes the aesthetic of this opener ^

guys I think Tame Impala is good :O

Desire Be Desire Go

hm, I don’t know entirely why, but that clipping guitar line sounds really good to me :)

I don't have the verve to belong to this dead side
Why I ever tried, I, I don't know
Oh, dare I face the real world?

jesus, even though they’re mixed and faded a bit into the background of the instrumental, the vocal lines here are surprisingly endearing and emotive

Alter Ego

alright alright, I really like the horn-style synths on the onset of this track, it’s an absolutely lovely, trance-like styling :D

That you are all you're meant to be
When the one from my dream
Is sitting right next to me
And I don't know what to do
Oh, alter ego

holy- that chorus is so damn well done, especially with how it pitches down over time, and everything that goes into this track

it all meshes so well together, I adore this track :)

Lucidity

god this feels so crunchy in my ears :O

oh shit, I love that second verse a lot- the playful chaotic nature of all of this feels so personal and timeless

I love the little come back into the thematic chorus of the track after a pause, it adds to the general feeling of the track as a whole I think

Why Won’t You Make Up Your Mind?

I like more defined, clear sounding synth that tracks back and forth by your head through this song- and I like how the drums feel marching forwards but not into anything dull or monotonous

especially with that crispy, rough guitar :D

Solitude Is Bliss

all of these tracks have felt so nice to the ear, each with a definable motif that keeps the general theme similar, while remaining–as a cohesive unit–stunningly interesting

and I love the stuttering swagger of this track, it feels so show-off-y in a likeable way :)

also just have to say: I have loved each and every bass instrumental in this whole LP so far (just to be reassuring)

Jeremy’s Storm

hmm, I’m liking the slightly jazz-y instrumentation behind the primary guitar line- and I like how this is in essence a long interlude

it splits things up nicely, and it serves as like a midpoint, transition-type interim before the remainder of the record

I guess it’s a good thing these sweeping, miniature synth pieces mesh so well with this increasingly diverse soundscape :)

Expectation

catchy and surprising little offset piece to start, you could say it has SUBVERTED MY EXPECTATION(S)

but yea anyways- this just seemed like the status quo in terms of this record (which is in no way a bad thing, it’s just the closest thing to a track that blends in for me so far)

I do like how the track fades and comes back for a bit; it’s nothing incredible, but it certainly upped the chaotic nature of this instrumentation in particular :O

and then it just ends on a simple chord lol

The Bold Arrow of Time

jesus, I was already impressed with the starting of this song, but that driving guitar riff and following solo are so crushing

the energy of this track is a fantastic lead up to the finish line for this LP, I love this track :)

huh, weird little interlude at the end, kinda touching but a bit of an interesting inclusion

Runaway, Houses, City, Clouds

okay I’m already getting excited for this track, the fade in is doing it for me

god I love every little addition that this given soundscape gives; those haunting synth melodies, the infectious guitar riff- it just feels so charming and ethereal altogether ^

But don't remind me of home
In case it isn't quite the same

and what a lyrically eminent ending- with all the instrumental that comes after, the fuzziness of this track is shockingly endearing and lovely :)

I Don’t Really Mind

woah… I didn’t know how this LP would possibly close out (especially after the last track) but a slightly more minimal, pop-oriented track is somehow really fitting

BUT OUCH my ears with that synth in the middle, wth :c

…it sounds kinda cool tho

thinking about it though- that’s a really aggressive, perfectly expected way to provide a visceral ending to all of this as an experience :)
