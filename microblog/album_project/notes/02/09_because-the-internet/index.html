notes for february 9

Because the Internet by Childish Gambino

The Library (Intro)

alright then, there is nothing to dissect here other than that's a cool sound

I. Crawl

damn okay- that's a great instrumental start to this record honestly with such a powerful beat :O

I don't think he has necessarily the most incredible flow or anything, but I do like Glover's inflection a lot :)

especially that first verse, that's pretty damn good ^

II. Worldstar

huh okay, I’m not as much of a fan of this… I think

this track goes in between over-bearing and perfectly slick in its styling for me; I like a lot of the aesthetic but some of the lines and a bit of the pounding beat can get to me

like that net twerk line is gross, wth

overall pretty good though, all things said

Dial Up

that’s a great little use for an interlude, very nice :)

I. The Worst Guys (feat. Chance the Rapper)

woah okay, nice intro so far ^

Tia and Tamera in my bed, I'm a smart guy
I ain't fucking with you n––s like apartheid (Nah)

bruhh… that line is the perfect amount of Gambino for me :0

this beat is honestly really damn good imo; it fits Glover’s cadence over it perfectly, and I love the melodic synth/horn sort of thing going on in the backdrop

that was a very subtle Chance feature; and that ending is so damn good, the sort of key change is very deserved :D

II. Shadows

I was a bit turned off by the start of this, but after the first verse and the chorus, I’m actually pretty sold on this track :)

I think everything here just sort of meshes in a really simplistic way which is why I’m able to like it; I think some of the bars are way too out there over the last couple tracks, but overall it’s just a good experience

and JESUS that outro was a surprising turn– that’s really damn good honestly

thank u mr thundercat

III. Telegraph Ave. (“Oakland” by Lloyd)

alright I think this track kinda starts out slow (not pace-wise, but in terms of likability), but it gets going pretty well

oh for sure; that third verse actually gets this track to a nice little crescendo that sort of tapers off for a while :)

roll credits ^

IV. Sweatpants

okay okay, here we go

I think this stretch of tracks that looks like act two—from The Worst Guys to 3005—might be the best set of cuts on this record as a whole especially because of this ending

(Fiskers don't make noise when they start up, just so you know)
Top of the holy totem
Ri-ri-ri-rich forever, a million was not the quota
My father owned half the MoMA and did it with no diploma

god this track is just a lovingly crafted, boastful set of rhymes and in-jokes— and I love it :)

is he eatin’ tho?

V. 3005

this is such a great ending to this set of five tracks, I adore this one so damn much

Assassins, I'm stabbed in the back of my cabin
Labrador yapping, I'm glad that it happened, I mean it
Between us, I think there's something special
And if I lose my mental, just hold my hand
Even if you don't understand, hol' up

I don’t think it’s necessarily the most interesting, compelling, or creatively adventurous cut… but jesus if it isn’t just a lovely experience to listen to :D

and jesus that beat behind it all is so damn good ^

he is very thirsty :O

Playing Around Before the Party Starts

okay that’s a nice little interlude to this; I’ve come to appreciate these interludes a lot since this album project started, and that’s a good placement ^

I. The Party

woah alright- this feels like a surprisingly significant shift from the first half of the record

alright at this first verse… my god okay this is damn good :O

I’m caught off guard by how quickly it shifts to the next track, but that was shockingly toned and a pretty good continuation honestly

II. No Exit

same for this track, it has the same sort of energy and track through this LP; I love Glover’s lower intonation over this deeply crushing beat :O

Death by Numbers

okay yea these transitions have turned this section into a great sort of continuity and sustained energy- I like this :)

I. Flight of the Navigator

this intro is a really somber, mellowed-out turn for the LP, and I honestly like the way it shifts, that’s really nice

I’m not as sold on the verses imo, but the chorus is honestly quite touching, and after the first chorus I do think that it keeps that same sort of feeling for me; very powerful track in its stylistic attributes and theme overall ^

I would say I think it keeps going for too long, but with the subtle changes that take place throughout the track and its runtime, it completely deserves the time it gets

II. Zealots of Stockholm (Free Information)

another pretty smooth transition, it feels like each section on the record does have a great consistency and sustained but unique theme to each of them ^

hmm okay, I'm not feeling this transition yet

alright that turned it around a bit, and that aggressive section was much more interesting than I was expecting

And wait 'til I'm walkin' in it
With a gun that they 3D printed and finish it
Kinison said, "If you gon' miss Heaven, why do it by two inches?"
Old money and new bitches

okay that ending was really good honestly- I'm just not really feeling the confusing and constant beat changes all that much; it's definitely great when it lands, but it just didn't as much as it went for it

III. Urn

honestly just a pretty good interlude sort of track; it didn't contribute much significantly, but it was a nice small break

I. Pink Toes (feat. Jhené Aiko)

honestly same with this track for me- it's a surprisingly well-structured, slightly mellow track, but it just doesn't serve as much of a significant purpose that I can tell

Aiko's contribution is really good though honestly, but this record is dropping in terms of total interest value I think

II. Earth: The Oldest Computer (The Last Night) [feat. Azealia Banks]

yea... I'm at the mid-section if this track and I'm just not feeling this :c

it doesn't feel as cohesive or compelling as the other parts of this record, which is kind of disappointing given the peaks this LP hit ^

I think this ending is at least a bit interesting and energetic, but it feels a bit raw and unseasoned ;/

III. Life: The Biggest Troll (Andrew Auernheimer)

I just start to feel like this record could have been better served as an EP, with a more cohesive, shorter runtime to benefit this theme

this track–while pretty good simply because of Glover’s mellow intonation in his bars–seems to just be a subtle change to a concept that’s already been well explored on the record

if this was the only ending, and those sustained synths were what closed this record out without the fluff before it, this would be much, much better imo

because this track is honestly pretty likeable, and has a good sort of tonal cohesion :)

it does kinda falter at the end though, but no matter- this LP has already sealed its end, and it was alright
