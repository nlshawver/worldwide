notes for february 22

self-titled by Rage Against The Machine

Bombtrack

great little bass line and DAMN what an introduction to a band :D

that descending tri-tone in the chorus is such a fantastic groove; I can't get the smile off my face :)

I don't know what it is about this track, just jesus christ I love it- everything always feels like it's at its peak instrumentally... until

Killing in the Name

that mf cowbell :O

while the message of this track is its most important aspect... holy shi- the package it comes in is an absolute metal masterpiece :D

the minimalism of the instrumental is by far its greatest strength; de la Rocha's inflection and general intonation throughout the whole track is insane too ^^

FUCK YOU I WON'T DO WHAT YOU TELL ME

Take the Power Back

damn okay, that funk bass introduction is a nice minor change-up; while the vocals here aren't as transcendentally powerful as the last track (and what is if we’re being honest), it's much more lyrically dense, which is a great point for it :)

the chorus is almost as catchy- and what a solo and transition into the spoken word sort of piece :O

I'm not as huge a fan of the way the end starts, but it's at least endearingly powerful ^^

Settle for Nothing

I wasn’t completely sold on that minute-long start to the track, but my god what a breakdown; completely jaw-dropping :O

these first four tracks are a stunning start for any album, but especially so for a debut LP of all things ^^

the complete polar opposites of the subtle, nodding verses into the crushing nature of the chorus is an amazing stylistic choice :D

Bullet in the Head

some of the little pieces of this record so far have reminded me of RHCP, and this track at its onset is the best example honestly lol

what an amazing guitar section on this track, each section has its own aesthetic and unique aspects that make it all such an earworm :)

OH MY GOD that mf breakdown after the midpoint is INSANE :OOO

I thought that second track was going to carry this LP, but god no, the rest of the tracklist does not need any help yet at all :D

Know Your Enemy

god that bridge with Maynard James Keenan is a must-needed respite in this powerful mash-up and application of all the sounds RATM is known for; I don’t think this track is as shockingly memorable as the others but it’s still got the same energy, same message… it’s just as potent :O

and jesus christ that last minute is more INSANE EARWORM SHIT :DD

Wake Up

more lyrically dense and intentionally stunning lyrics on this track ^

I think this is the only track on this LP so far where it’s only mainly the strings that stick out; the rest is still good of course, but it’s just a little under the insane curve this record set out with ^^

okay that half-way point is a incredible change-up for this cut, I do like how focused it is on the guitar and bass :)

while it doesn’t have nearly the same inherent stature of Killing in the Name, my god that ending is just as potent :O

Fistful of Steel

I don’t really have much else to say here; message is focused and stiff, instrumentals are earworm bangers… every chorus is insanely haunting and memorable, and the spirit of intensity is indescribable ^^

AHHHHHHHH COME ON, HOW‽

Township Rebellion

not much else to say here either; it’s still just as hungry and eccentric, but some of the elements are a tinge stale now

the guitar & bass yet again are the shining stars of this cut in all honesty, it’s such an appealing, threatening kind of instrumental ^^^

WHEN IGNORANCE REIGNS, LIFE IS LOST

that’s by far one of the best lines of the whole record, god damn :OO

Freedom

that was a great little transition, it feels like the track cut was more-so a moment of silence that re-introduces a very similar, yet uniquely distinctive instrumental style :)

the cohesion of this track’s arc is a bit more confusing, and yes a good amount of the that initial luster is kinda gone; a good instrumental cannot be ignored tho ^

I feel like there could be no different ending to this LP honestly, that chaos in the whole set being mercilessly destroyed is perfect for it all :D
