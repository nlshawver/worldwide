notes for february 3

channel ORANGE by Frank Ocean

Start

gotta love the flair of all the nostalgic video game sound bytes :)

Thinkin Bout You

alright, five out of five track already; this is an absolute treat

god everything about this song just feels incredible, I absolutely love the very slight, simple instrumental in all these amazing vocal lines :D

Fertilizer

okay I didn’t think too sandwich bread interlude/transition pieces would work well, but that added a cool bit of separation in all honesty

Sierra Leone

ooh I love the lyrical back and forth, that’s a wonderful sort of feeling :)

and that chorus, with Ocean’s vocals swimming through the instrumental is such a great choice, and the aesthetic is produces is amazing for such a short track

Sweet Life

I didn’t initially think that first verse was all that special, but seriously it flows so damn well into that fantastic chorus :O

I think I might be so used to Frank Ocean’s style now it doesn’t feel as special as it should- but I know it’s absolutely lovely, and this track is the little peak of the first third of this record so far :)

Not Just Money

especially with the theme of the last track, I love all these interludes, and they fit so well :)

Super Rich Kids (feat. Earl Sweatshirt)

okay damn, we’re already on this track :D

that Earl Sweatshirt feature is seriously such a great back and forth, and the offsetting energy and tonality of their voices is amazing- not even mentioning the meaning of the lyrics and how well it fits into this album’s thematic path

I love this track, definitely my favorite so far (even if the record just seems to keep improving over itself)

Pilot Jones

oh my god that subtle, threatening bass; it gets so low :O

it’s just kind of a subtle, meaningfully somber track, and it’s just so well done– even if compared to the other tracks it doesn’t stick out as much

Crack Rock

the introduction of this track certainly does though; I love the instantly punchy drum set, with the plucky synth under it

and of course Ocean’s vocals :)

Hittin' stones in glass homes
You're smokin' stones in abandoned homes
You hit them stones and broke your home

that’s such a weird, unsettling reprieve… and the rest of the track fits the underlying theme well, as usual ^

jesus, and that last hook is so striking :O

Pyramids

those backwards drums- and my GOD those synths, what the hell

AHHHH that first verse is an absolute jam, that’s seriously shocking how well this comes together

the layers of intrigue and meaning behind this track is jaw-dropping; there’s no other way to say it :O

Got rubies in my damn chain
Whip ain't got no gas tank but it still got woodgrain
Got your girl working for me

that set of lines alone has like three meanings all at once… it’s indescribable how insane this man is at crafting a lyrical story in such a compelling, multi-faceted way

Lost

damn, it seemed like there was no way to transition out of that last track, and while I think this track feels completely different to Pyramids, this does feel like a worthy, well-done follow-up :)

also that chorus is an absolute jam, such a wonderful aesthetic ^

White (feat. John Mayer)

damn okay, that super synth-like, buzzing bass line is amazing :D

that’s a great little break, I appreciate that

Monks

alright alright, what a punchy, expressive introduction to this track

god, and those floaty synths at the third verse are lovely- and OOH that bass line with Frank’s vocals

it’s really just the same good stuff over and over again, just with these nice little differences in aesthetic and instrumental details

Bad Religion

okay the first time I heard this I wasn’t terribly impressed, and it felt like a sort of transitionary track- but knowing the meaning of everything he’s singing, it’s genuinely touching and emotional knowing how it all meshes together

and especially how this instrumental fits together, and the progression it takes on throughout the track; it’s just impressive and lyrically beautiful, that’s all

Pink Matter (feat. André 3000)

I like the subtlety of this track, ya know >v>

but seriously, this is absolutely amazing- I can’t keep saying the same things about these tracks

GOD I love the 3k feature; just like Earl his aesthetic clashes in a beautiful, chaotic way, even if their topic of interest is the exact same

this track made me appreciate this record so much more than I did before, and for that I can’t help but make this my favorite track on the album so far :)

Forrest Gump

and this just feels like a subtle, touching lead down to the end of the record

the layered vocals are a lovely treat for the ending of this in particular, and everything is lovely

it’s all lovely :)

End

oh I love this so damn much; the rain being the focal point is the perfect setting, and dude- when the car opens, my god it just feels like one of those nights where you don’t know what to do exactly as a whole, but you just do what you want right in the moment, without regard for how it looks on the outside

it’s all on the inside; that’s the aesthetic I get, and it’s wonderful

jesus christ that break was such an unusual, transcendent sort of trip

I can’t explain it, but it just feels so right to have a moment like that, it’s magnificent

oh yea, and the instrumental feels like a soulful minecraft rendition with how playful and hopeful it comes off as :)

I don’t know how I feel about the pitched down Tyler vocals, but I love the line it ends on:

I free-fall off the hill again
Let's see where I land, I'm like Gilligan
Um, I trust you, Golden, for wed ring
I'm just hopin' that you don't turn my neck green

and it’s just the beach and this island, and that’s all
