notes for february 12

self-titled by LCD Soundsystem

Daft Punk Is Playing at My House

huh alright, was only sort of expecting a style like this; it’s much more skeletal than I was expecting in all honesty

I do really like this persistent guitar riff though, and the cowbell is a lovely touch lol (:

honesty that’s a pretty good opener, I can kind of understand the appeal going into the fact that this is their first LP

Too Much Love

huh alright- I really like the bass-line and primary instrumental, but the vocals are actually much less interesting that I they came off as the first time around

it feels like Daft Punk’s homework, in that there’s a dedication to one sort of sound for a long period of time before changing anything up—which can be either surprisingly well-made, or just a repetitive slog… and I’m really leaning towards the latter for this track :c

I do think it gets much more appealing and interesting after the second half though, with the chaotic drum line behind the stuttering synth lead ^

Tribulations

okay okay, I do really like the driving synth change-up opening this track though, it’s the perfect level of overblown and minimal at the same time

I think the vocals are alright, and they get better with time as you get more and more into the meat of the song ^

ooh and I really like the fadeout of the guitar that leaves the bare instrumental behind it, that was a nice little moment; and the ending is even better, I think this is a much better continuation from the opening track, and lends to this style much better :)

Movement

oh shit, I love this energy :O

the vocals are surprisingly interesting, and I absolutely love the incredible drum and guitar line that explodes into the track :D

somehow this is my favorite track so far, I just adore the little switch-up it provided :)

gotta love the slow-down at the end too ^

Never as Tired as When I’m Waking Up

hmm, another interesting switch-up in styling; these vocals are much more compelling and deeply calming than the rest of the LP has given me

okay this is already so good, this is a great follow-up, and might be another high point on the record for me given the progression up to this point ^

god I love the relatively simplistic guitar work around three minutes in, it matches and adds so much to the aesthetic of this track, it’s absolutely lovely :)

new favorite, and this midsection of the record is much better to my ears

On Repeat

I thought I’d be turned off by the long duration of this track, but this is honestly a bit of a sleeper; it has a great mix of general energy along with how the instrumental works with Murphy’s vocals, and I think it compliments the stylistic choices of the last two tracks really well :)

it obviously does get a bit repetitive, but the styling of the track keeps it from being monotonous in my opinion- and it’s a good track leading into the final third of the (standard) album runtime

Thrills

ooh, that’s a weird, crushing instrumental going into this, and I don’t know how I feel about it in all honesty

I do like the mixing of the vocals that come in, especially by the 1:15 mark- and I like the slight instrumental change right after (and then through to the end) :O

I think this one just gets better with time, even if the main instrumental choice is so harsh to my ears; the start was just a shock

Disco Infiltrator

wow okay, I already love this :D

the slick instrumental is a great touch, and the vocals overall on this record have gotten better and better throughout the tracklist imo :)

that was just an overall great track, I love the ending a lot too ^^

Great Release

huh, I like the slightly mellow, yet positive intonation of this final track- and when the vocals finally come in it feels like a really great moment :)

can’t not love the huge strings that appear behind this whole soundscape too ^

honestly somehow a bit underwhelming, and it certainly didn’t seem as transcendent of a release as I was expected– but it still closes off the primary record in a great place, and I came to appreciate this album much more than I expected :)

and I guess he left the room :O
