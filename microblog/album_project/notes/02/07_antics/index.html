notes for february 7

Antics by Interpol

Next Exit

huh, I kinda like that introduction- but I fear the same things I didn't mesh with as much on their first record are just showing up again

but all said and done, that's a pretty good start to an old indie rock record

Evil

ooh, I like that instrumental a lot, but I do not like a lot of the intonation of Banks and his voice over top this instrumentation- it just sounds unpleasantly nasal at times, and I can't overlook that :c

which is genuinely a shame cause the drums on this are really nice

Narc

alright alright, I like this track a lot more, and all the instrumental work compliments Banks's vocality much better in my opinion

that bass line and cymbal work are the most satisfying to me ^

and oooh that subtle synth harmony underneath all of this–which comes out when the drums are absent–is really nice :)

Take You on a Cruise

okay this track is surprisingly good, I love the overall aesthetic with the galloping tone to the guitars, and the drums serve their job perfectly :O

AND WOAH that little mid-track stutter–while a bit expected–gave this track a lovely energy at the midpoint that made its longer track length feel required and worthwhile

this is definitely my favorite track so far (and I really need to listen to their debut LP again)

Slow Hands

ooh yea, and that was a very good continuation of the last track; it didn't seem nearly as unique but I love the general aesthetic of the last two tracks

Not Even Jail

damn- this bass intro :O

and for sure, these last three tracks have been much more of an upturn from my thoughts on the first two- and in general from Interpol

Public Pervert / C’mere

I don’t have a whole lot to say about these two tracks, but the fact that I have so little to say is both a good and bad thing

I think each of them was charming, and interesting to an extent, but are just spoiled by what’s bracketing the record; I will say that a lot of the bass and drum lines are lovely to listen to, and the subtle detailing is important to how much I think these tracks are an improvement over the other tracks here

overall two pretty good tracks in the middle; this record deserves a closer listen than I gave it today, but I can’t shake the feeling initiated by the overall aesthetic presented

Length of Love

okay this is absolutely lovely- definitely my favorite overall track on the album, even if some of the others earlier on had more interesting individual details

this, as a sum of its parts, with the careful intonation and attention to everything that makes it up, is charming—even if the details are a bit fuzzy

A Time to Be So Small

this record really should have ended on length of love, or at the very least had a different aesthetic for this track

I don’t know what all it is about it, and maybe it’s a fleeting feeling reserved for this night when I’m reviewing it, but I seriously didn’t like the ending, and I think it spoiled a bit the increase in value and quality of the record up the ninth track :c
