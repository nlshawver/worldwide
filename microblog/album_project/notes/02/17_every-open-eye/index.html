notes for february 17

Every Open Eye by CHVRCHΞS

Never Ending Circles

jesus what an opener, I probably should’ve expected what I already knew what was coming given the last LP lol

I do really like this beat and the staccato nature of it (and the nice little vocal samples behind it at times), but sometimes those vocals kinda throw me for a loop ^

okay- but actually for a really good reason, I like this a lot already :)

the ~bass~ :O

honestly this carries the same sort of chaotic, carefully composed energy as their last opener, The Mother We Share

Leave a Trace

ahhh okay- I wasn’t feeling this at first, but god the combination of that calmer instrumental and that swap between pleasant and shredding vocal inflection on the chorus is so nice :D

her voice with her intonation is so charismatic, I adore the sort of inflection she has with the little zest her accent adds :)

just an overall lovely track to listen to, great follow-up ^^

Keep You on My Side

another really good beat switch up- and again :O

it’s almost a motif of all of their tracks, where the track sort of starts alright, a bit of a change into something more mellow explodes into something loaded with chaotic beauty

this is another one of those examples, even if I don’t think this has reached the peaks of their last record (so far)

it almost feels like a veil is being lifted sometimes when everything comes together, that's really damn well-made :)

Make Them Gold

honestly this one is a bit of a meh for me- it's nice and had a shiny exterior as always, but I'm not feeling it as much

okay the vocals after the first chorus are really good tho, that sort of small drag turned into something nice

this is one of those deep cuts which are really nice additions to the record, but not nearly as memorable as the main pieces

Clearest Blue

there's the name drop (:

the bass shines a lot on this track for me, I'm liking the slightly different progression of it as a whole; it's given much more space to stretch through this mellow (for chvrches) sort of theme for a while

holy shit... yea I don't think that hit the way I was expecting it, but god that slightly off-kilter, pulsating chorus instrumental is insane :O

High Enough to Carry You Over

this vocal part by...apologies...not the lead singer- it's honestly much better and more compelling than the track that was included on their debut :)

unfortunately the only downside is that it serves a sort of mid-point slog forward; it's not bad by any stretch of the word, but it’s just a subtler, transitionary piece

Empty Threat

this goes back to the exciting sort of aesthetic—and maybe it’s just because a bit of the luster of their last LP is gone—but this takes a bit for me to be genuinely interested in

and by the time I’m invested, it’s already the end of the bridge, and the song is fading out already…

I think I’m just having a moment

Down Side of Me

yea definitely a moment, especially when I already really like this track with just the introduction :O

god… yup I love this- Mayberry’s vocals in a more toned-down—yet still charismatically synth-heavy track—are just so likeable to my ears with her inflection and styling :D

Playing Dead

I do like the very, very retro-sounding synths here, and that chorus 100% slaps hard :)

but I still think a bit of the luster really is gone… I’m not sure, I think I just enjoyed their debut a good amount more than this LP

Bury It

the careful simplicity of the start of this cut is really appealing- and my GOD that little after-chorus instrumental is wonderfully ecstatic and comfortable in its craziness :D

I think this honestly reaches the closest to the original love I had for their original LP (and I kind of hate how much I’m comparing them)

because in all honesty, this album is really good… I just for some reason or another cannot shake that initial impression I had :c

Afterglow

while I do feel like this is expected, and somehow a bit misplaced, I still like the dedication and consistency with the mellow cuts throughout this tracklist :)

vocals are beautiful as always; and somehow touchingly somber at the end… this was, I think, much better than I’m giving it credit ^^
