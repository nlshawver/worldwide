mini notes for thumbs

  • great panning to start this mixtape, and a wild name of absolutions in the hottentot supercluster :)
    • honestly, while I didn’t really match well into the weird, off-kilter cadence of driver’s verses at the onset, the meaning of what he’s saying still seems to overshadow the flow- it takes a lot to get into the flow I think; it’s implicitly more complex and a bit more obtuse, just to get you to hear it all
    • zeroh had a great ending verse though to be honest, he fits in with a bit of a different perspective I find very valuable in this opening cut :))
  • yea that’s a really staggered, almost unorganized flow with the first verse on black labor— but god damn the way he shows he can fit right in with the beat is incredible :D
    • yea from the hook onwards its an insane instrumental with the bright, cheery synths (and slight bass) with busdriver’s sometimes intentionally accented inflection c:
    • it’s not always fantastic, but the way he rides the beat in an intriguing, unique way is something I can’t overlook ^^
    • and that’s ignoring the great lyrical content, the female sample at the end, and the crazed audio clip closing it out :o
  • while I sort of like that driving, squealing synth lead in the background, I don’t see a lot in the lyrical content to back up such a beat on hyperbolic 2
    • not bad tho, just a bit of a downturn ^
  • okay woah, I really like that hook on much, but some of the way it comes together is just pretty good; I have to be honest tho in saying “with the creation myth, the bits between your ears ain't jailbroken” is a great dig at religion in the middle of some weirdly abstract bars in the first verse
    • and the quality stays high the whole song; it might’ve just been first listen jitters, this track is great :)
  • woah and I was not expecting the follow-up of ministry of the torture couch; it has a great little half-subtle instrumental under some pretty great, lush lyrical passages :)
    • I feel it’s not as significant as the previous or next tracks, but it’s certainly a fantastic way to bridge the gap between each :))
    • that hook is really mf catchy too, I’ll always like the way busdriver sounds and the way he fits into stuff like this deep synth instrumental behind him :D
    • jesus christ- I wasn’t all that huge about that second hook (even if I agree with it), but the way it ends up bookending an esoteric, beautiful third verse about downtrodden veterans, institutional racism and control, and government b.s. is actually really fucking great :OO
  • okay I don't know what it is, whether it's nostalgia or something similar, but I absolutely adore the instrumental and mixing of worlds to run- it's genuinely in my top fifty (maybe ten) songs ever made, it just connects so well w me :OOO
    • and even though it's a bit of a shift from the starting tracks prior, it's a wonderful moment for the tape :))
    • everyone is incredible, milo's starting part is amazing, this was the first hook I ever heard from paak and it's stunning, and busdriver's part is the smoothest I've ever heard him c:
    • 5/5, beautiful cut ^^^
  • and while I don't have much to say about the following cuts (it's a bit of a struggle to stay on things w vacation rn), but I think it was overall something that complimented the record
  • genuinely this is a tape that merits more time to listen in detail, along with perfect hair (as this serves pretty much as a compliment/supplement/contrast to it); del’s feature is incredible, and each cut after worlds to run was at least intriguing :)
  • great record, and think of the rating as a temporary mark of appreciation for both this and perfect hair c:
