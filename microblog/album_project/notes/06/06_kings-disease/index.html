mini notes for king's disease

  • woah, great way to start the record with that title track; the hit-boy production is already pretty great, with that vocal sample provided some rly beautiful harmonics c:
    • the lyrical content is pretty damn good too, I especially liked the sprinkling in of black positivity with "the stupidest part of africa produced blacks that started algebra", mixed all in with some equally intriguing poetry :)
  • oooh great vocal sample into a powerful asf beat :O
    • damn this track with that minor beat switch and the shimmering yet effortlessly high-quality bars :))
    • it's a bit of a simple track, but it's lovely in its own sense ^
  • and while the beat of car #85 isn't all too fantastical, the storytelling and thematic images seen in that first verse is a great moment on the record; it's a personal look yet again for nas on what seems to be some pretty personal life stories :o
    • it's a nice comedown from the previous track, to a certain extent at least
  • ultra black as the lead single is such a good choice; that beat that just begs for the verse cadence to match gives a great sense of both careful confidence, along with just a wonderful voice to the whole cut :D
    • absolutely fantastic, almost inspiring track, just in the first foray into this record c:
  • alright alright, and 27 summers is a nice, short little banger to follow it up; it has a great starting verse and chorus, and that's about all it wrote :)
  • replace me is just a bit of a weird mess after it though- the beat certainly isn't bad, but it's just a bit of a turnaround from the previous tracks on the record, w both don toliver and big sean feeling weird as features here :/
    • don has a pretty alright hook, but big sean does not fit at all imo
  • ooh I know that instrumental on til the war is won :o
    • honestly it's a pretty passable track; there's just not a ton that shines through all that explicitly
    • but it's a pleasant track about women in life, the honor held to them by nas, and that general positive energy; it's just not shockingly remarkable or anything like that
  • alright wow, I didn't think I'd like nas and paak together nearly as much as I do, but all bad is a pretty damn good ballad to come back from the previous track; there's a bit there to grab onto, and just enough for me to enjoy it pretty well :)
  • damn, and while it's a short, almost forgettable track after all bad and its failed love themes, the definition is a nice little boom bap interlude c:
  • full circle is definitely the mid-to-late album high point for me, especially with the surprisingly great az and cormega features; it's wonderfully indulgent, and overall made really damn well with more hit-boy mastery :))
    • and I didn't forget- foxy brown has a great feature as well, and serves as a nice aesthetic and thematic contrast with her own bars ^^
    • dre outro? :o
  • and while it has just an okay transition into 10 points, damn the instrumental that comes through with the trumpets surrounding you and that deep bass... great follow-up track, if it's only just a bit indulgent :)
    • really glad there's three verses too, better space to spread his wings :O
    • this and full circle are a hell of a duo ngl :D
  • okay goddamn the cure might be the most detailed, interesting, and almost soulful cut here so far :OO
    • the mccartney/lennon line to start, comparing him and pac/biggie/etc. respectively is a fantastic way to follow a paradox of the industry and common perception
    • and then it's just constant bars of intrigue and life lessons and experiences :o
    • the MOMA line on the second verse, along w generational curses, mc shan and marley marl, and the mf rick james behavior lines are all highlights ^^
  • "mets hat in the sky like bobby shmurda" damn lmao
    • honestly this is not the best outro track, but on its own it's another indulgent track w some not bad features w this beat ^
    • yea fivio is just pretty good, but ferg shines on this beat (rather obviously too)
    • I think this could've been served better in a different part of the tracklist, especially since ferg technically closes out the album, but whatever- it's still one of the better tracks I think
