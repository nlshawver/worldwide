mini notes for you can’t kill me

  • damn, beautiful sounding opener to open this LP, and to end off web :)
  • while invited is kind of just sitting there for a while, and the build-up is good but a little too dragged out, when those unique drum patches and the rest of the instrumental come in, it’s a really neat experience c:
    • it’s just a shame that it lasts for less than the final half of the track, and just fizzles out- it would’ve been cool to see it develop even further I think ^
  • ooh I already love history; that driving drum beat behind those spearing synth harmonies behind shake’s voice :oo
    • it’s just overall a really great, expressive cut, with a fantastic switch-up into the ending, resulting in a great, personable verse to close out on :D
  • that deep, rumbling bass line on medicine is a really compelling addition under those previous lines about being absorbed and entranced by someone serving as her ‘medicine’; it’s interesting in both concept and execution, and a nice follow-up to the previous explosion :)
  • oh my god that beat & key change on skin and bones :OO
    • that was genuinely like a mind-fuck moment, I’m just kinda blown away in the way that played out- it made a bit of the lyrical content carry more weight (especially that outro), and it was just a bit of a magical moment there :))
  • damn- and while it didn’t connect nearly as hard, blue velvet is a nice, thematic continuation of the previous track ^
  • cocoon is a small bop though; I feel like that’s a lot of what’s here tbh, just small snippets of bombast, surrounded in most of these tracks by atmospheric, sometimes ethereal soundscapes on both sides
    • it’s an interesting experience, but one that I think may start to run thin soon…
  • rather in-and-out listen on body, where I know it’s a pretty decent track, but it just kind of floats there before leaving unfortunately :/
  • interesting choice on the guitar for wine & spirits; I think the imagery is a bit more interesting than past tracks here, even if the whole experience just leaves me faded
    • it’s not a bad track in this style, and a pretty good way to lead off this final leg of the record;
  • come back home is an expansive track- and while I think at times it can hit a bit of a peak for this part of the record, it was another one that sort of lost its weight right after it had finished unfortunately…
  • vibrations feels like a slightly over-simplistic cut to follow, but honestly it does nothing too bad- it just sort of meanders in the place it was left by the previous cut, and offers a pretty compelling instrumental on the hook
    • I may be over-critical at times, because honestly that first half of the record was pretty good, and from what I’ve heard this second half has just been connective tissue to that experience ^
  • purple walls was honestly pretty good too- it was nothing monumental, but in the space it occupies it did a pretty good job at conveying a sort of ethereal quality (even if just for a moment)
  • stay was a fine follow-up… I’m sorry, I’m just running out of ways to describe this album tbh :c
  • beautiful closer though, I have to be honest; from that driving rhythm mix, to the alluring vocal performance, and the horns to close it all out… yea that was pretty special ngl :)
