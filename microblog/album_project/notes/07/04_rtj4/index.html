mini notes for rtj4

  • what a mf opener to this record; yankee and the brave has such a huge, all-encompassing instrumental behind it that’s genuinely distracted… but the bars creeping around it are incredible :O
  • and while the hook is a bit goofy, ooh la la is a fantastic fusion of sound with greg nice & dj premier providing their services well (in very different ways :)
    • that final verse from mike too :oo
  • god damn that beat on out of sight, executed perfectly with that shared verse, w/ some great one-liners and incredible messaging hidden within it :O
    • and damnn okay, 2 chainz is insane on his verse, he wraps it up in such a likable, instantly recognizable way- and I just love his inflection on the verse :)
  • holy calamafuck is the best possible written reaction to the song itself- the first part of the track is already an insane earworm with incredible lyricism BUT THAT MF BEAT SWITCH :OOOO
    • genuinely jaw-dropping, el-p is a genius with his expansive verse to start that second half; mike is equally hungry on his verse, and I seriously can’t dig up enough praise for this track
    • it’s magical to me, it’s executed perfectly- z-kicks’s chorus is fucking incredible
    • FIRE! AHHHHH THAT SHIT IS SO GOOD
  • goonies vs. e.t. is an amazing follow-up too; the focus on specifically environmental decay and the failure of our species to do meaningful change is a catastrophically intense focal point on the driving, intense instrumental behind both el-p and killer mike’s transcendent lyricism :oo
    • my god mike’s verse to close it out :O
    • continuously impressed, I have no words…
  • “if even one scrap of what jesus taught connected, you’d feel different” god damn el :O
    • gangsta boo on this track is an amazing choice, she matches the energy level perfectly on that hook c:
    • shivers at the “i can’t breathe” line; this was before floyd, and it still, still, still applies…
    • “never forget in the story of jesus, the hero was killed by the state” there is not enough praise that can be levied the way of this record
    • gangsta boo on the ending, I love it ;)
  • ju$t might only be the second best collab with de la rocha, but the inclusion of pharrell and that deep, murky beat behind it all is absolutely incredible w the dense, intricate lyricism (as usual)
    • “master of these politics, you swear that you got options / master of opinion ‘cause you vote with the white collar” god damn pharrell :O
    • great closing verse from zack too (again, as usual)
  • never look back is honestly a great moment after the politically charged messaging of… all of the record up to this point; it’s a bit more of an introspective, personal experience that has them reflecting on what got them to wear they are
    • and it’s a great experience to be taken along with, esp w/how genuinely crushing some of the lines are (especially from mike in his starting verse, my god)
    • I also have to compliment how great these transitions are across the whole LP, damn c:
  • the toussaint l’ouverture reference :ooo
    • the ground below might be one of the best deep cuts on the record; both the charging instrumental and the desperation for survival and greatness in mike’s and el-p’s verses respectively are awe-inspiring ^^^
  • the more understated yet still enveloping beat on pulling the pin is incessantly fantastical- and el-p’s far-reaching beginning verse is unimaginably great… and mike’s isn’t far behind ^
    • both provide acutely specific and crushing criticisms of systematic issues with christian imagery, and it’s just such a deconstruction of all that makes the vitriol on this LP meaningful and so damn worthwhile—
  • I have nothing better to say about a few words for the firing squad… it’s a legendary closer, and you just have to listen through it in context
    • “I need a husband more than the world need another martyr”
    • “god blessed me to redeem her in my thoughts, words, and my actions”
  • the fact that this album is cyclical is all the more terrifying, despite the theatrics closing it out in a heroic, western-inspired sort of way
  • what a legendary protest album, and what a peak to RTJ’s discography…
