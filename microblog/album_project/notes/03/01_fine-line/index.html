mini notes for fine line

  • these first three tracks are an incredible start, and form the album into almost an instant pop hit
  • I do like the energy and aesthetic shown off in the mid-section, but starting with Lights Up it feels like a lot of the the themes are very surface-level unfortunately; it’s still a pleasant, relieving sort of sound
  • sunflower, vol. 6 is an incredible, cute kind of return to that pop sound, and it’s absolutely lovely… maybe we rly should listen to dancehall :)
  • sort of falls apart at the end honestly- a lot of the instrumentals and vocals are shown off well, but they don’t compliment the ending style as well as I’d have hoped
  • okay some elements are still rly good, I eat some of my words; the peaks of this LP are near pop perfection, and honestly the title track is a really great cap to the record :)
