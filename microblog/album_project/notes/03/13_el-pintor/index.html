mini notes for el pintor

  • I’ve got it: I don’t think any of the songs on the three interpol albums I’ve listened to are bad, I’m just rarely in the mood for this type of rock I think
  • because all the rage back home is a great introductory track, and I love the styling with the floating guitar line throughout the whole track… tbf I think I may have been too harsh to those last albums, they deserve more credit than I gave them
    • my desire is genuinely a pretty good follow-up too :)
    • god I need to give this band more credit; I think a critique I will never make is that these records ever felt creatively barren or tired, because there’s always something there to latch onto and feel attached too
  • and while my notes dry up (time restraints and increased interest in articulating my points for the primary image post) moments throughout the record continue to impress me in a great kind of way
    • honestly all of the tracks seem interesting in one way or another, with cuts like my blue supreme and breaker 1 having a great amount of interesting energy (even if I personally love my blue supreme much more)
    • it might be temporary enjoyment for this record specifically, but if it means that I gain a new appreciation for interpol’s older discography, I don’t mind that much
