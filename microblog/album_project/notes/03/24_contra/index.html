mini notes for contra

  • I’m not sure how much I fully remember about my experience with vampire weekend’s debut, but this feels like a less chamber-y, nice sort of start with horchata :)
  • white sky feels like a pleasant, not too excited or over-bearing pop track… I like it, but I’m not seeing the huge hype around this record yet in all honesty—
  • that futura line on holiday is incredible tho :D
    • holiday is my favorite track here so far, I’m starting to get into it a little more :)
  • wow okay, california english is somehow explosive compared to what’s been here so far- I kinda like it :)
    • the huge reverb over some complex lines is both endearing and confusingly chaotic ^
  • taxi cab is… I don’t even know, it’s a loving, saddening ballad of sorts about such a confusing, close relationship- and it’s so relatable because it’s not either person’s fault, it just didn’t work out…
    • best track here so far in terms of meaning ^^
    • the repeated “like an aristocrat” callback is really nice, like they’ve gone through everything, and now it’s a joking, friendly sort of ending about those same experiences they bonded over…
  • run is a fitting progression, I love it :)
  • and damn if cousins isn’t incredible in its guitar riffs and chaotic intonation— but woah… the instrumental change to giving up the gun :O
    • this four track run from taxi cab to here is insane ^^
  • the way this kind of grows and morphs with its sounds and instruments feels really nice- it’s nothing outstanding or shuddering, but it’s a great, pleasant sound :)
  • huh, diplomat’s son starts in a way that’s both completely expected, yet still unique and interesting—good stuff
    • I don’t think it’s my favorite track here by a decent margin, but the chopped and slowed instrumentation halfway through changes things up in a great way—this is a very interesting, explorative cut :)
  • i think ur a contra feels like a beautiful sort of ending… I’m still not completely sold on the great stature this album has been given by other publications, but it’s certainly one of vampire weekend’s best (and I liked it more than the debut)
    • :)
