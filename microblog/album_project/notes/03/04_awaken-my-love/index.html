mini notes for "awaken, my love!"

  • wow, very pleasantly surprised by these two starting tracks :O
  • the very obvious blending of jazz, neo soul, funk, and r&b seems to honestly come off without hitch, uninhibited by any lacking creative skills from Glover :)
  • jesus christ Redbone is an instant classic :D
    • it’s such a good mid-section track that compliments the rest of the tracklist (even if I think the track before and after it are meh)
  • especially after… I am really not feeling this :/
  • Zombies and California just felt off and a bit out-of-place, but Terrified is a great little return to what I knew this record was capable of achieving; the instrumentation (especially that bass line and choice of instruments) and vocals are great :)
  • have to give a shout out to a lot of the transitions here too, they’re really damn good ^
  • and wow, Baby Boy is a wonderful deep cut here; it contains every single element that makes Redbone so good, and completes that same sort of feeling in a unique way :D
  • every single track after Redbone besides California was pretty great in its own way; and the whole LP had a winding, confusing, but pleasant ending
