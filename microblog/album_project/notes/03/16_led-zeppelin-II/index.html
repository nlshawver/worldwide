mini notes for led zeppelin II

  • what an absolutely heavy classic to start off this record… I don’t think this hits the conscious ping in my brain that communication breakdown does, but I think this cut is the closest equivalent on this LP :D
  • what is and what should never be is a compelling cut after the opening track, and while I don’t think it’s incredible in their discography, the small instrumentation at times (like that gong two thirds through) has a great attention to detail :)
  • I feel like there’s not much to say about the lemon song and thank you, they just feel like perfect inclusionary cuts in the mix of this record
    • that’s a serious problem with reviewing records so old… I can’t tell whether I just don’t like the sound in general, or it feels just pretty good, or if the bias of having such a swath of newer music influences greatly what I think of old records
    • I feel like both are kinda true
  • at least I know heartbreaker is an absolute banger :D
  • ohhh living loving maid is this song :O
  • jesus christ this span of tracks is so insanely iconographic- ramble on and moby dick back to back is just amazing :D
    • the fact that it's just one huge drum fill & solo makes it so much better, I legitimately laughed out loud when the rest of the instruments came in on that crash :))
  • I feel like the ending track starts out as a sleeper, but morphs really nicely into a great crescendo ^^
