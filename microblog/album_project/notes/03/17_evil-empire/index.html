mini notes for evil empire

  • jesus christ what an absolutely stellar start to this LP... bulls on parade is without a doubt an s-tier classic that literally never gets old :D
  • vietnow felt like a bit of a middling yet smashing cut, but being layered between bulls and revolver is a great combination- because revolver is amazing :)
  • oh my god and somehow snakecharmer leaps a bound over top of its predecessor; that was incredible :O
  • I don't think I appreciated nearly as much the aspects of tire me, but the strains and general intonation of de la rocha's voice is always great ^^
    • oh and it's relatively similar for down rodeo, just this time with the main chorus and bridge; the instrumentation is great as usual :)
  • jesus and without a face is a great follow-up, I think the constant guitar somehow ties this together chaotically; it's a rly good thematic choice for such a cut
    • also sorry idrk what happened, but I listened to the following tracks off the book; I thought they were all good of course, but if any part of this record deserves a re-listen from me it’s them
    • I think I got a little phased out, and didn’t truly listen deeply to what was happening
