mini notes for fleet foxes

  • sun it rises is genuinely a fantastic start to this LP, I'm loving every part of this :D
    • the combo of subtle and wonderfully bombastic everything (vocals, guitar, instrumental) morph in a great way ^
  • ngl I was worried with white winter hymnal at first because of the almost church-vibe of it, but it very quickly dashed any fears with a unique energy to match that inherently catchy groove :)
  • while I'm not as blown away by ragged wood, the way it stretches and fills its runtime is amazing ^^
    • okay the lyrics are actually incredible- I love this one now :D
  • tiger mountain peasant song is a wonderfully lovely cut that almost feels like a spoken word journey- another really good track here ^
  • quiet houses, counterintuitively, is a great upbeat comeback from tiger; I love it :)
  • I don't know what it is about it, but he doesn't know why is another fantastic track; everything here has a great sensibility in its specific styling, and I'm adoring every moment :D
  • while not necessarily a massive build-up, the interlude of heard them stirring is great in its own right ^
  • I don't think your protector was a standout hit, but yet again it's a good song in a good placement on this LP ^
  • meadowlarks though is a stunningly somber cut, I love the layered vocals throughout w the subtle guitar lines :))
  • this is one of the few albums that never has a lacking moment, nothing that feels like it could've been skipped or altered along the journey
  • oh my god the buildup on blue ridge mountains is absolutely beautiful :D
    • it's like the perfect release from the emotions shown on the previous tracks- it's so loving and incredibly attuned :)
  • the perfect ending- a countertop conversation filled with emotive back-and-forths and detailed stories
  • I may have a bit of recency bias on my discovery bias on fleet foxes... but I think the rating this gets is absolutely deserved :)
