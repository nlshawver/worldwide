an appreciation post for RSS

I'm not sure whether this will fall on deaf ears, but I seriously just wanted to say that, even though I grew up in a time when RSS feeds were already dying a bit, and the actual medium was becoming much less common, I still find the idea perfect for coordinating stuff you want to read from all the garbage you don't want to see. It's so simplistic, and there's so many good methods of viewing and enjoying the content you like.

I'll be honest in saying I don't know a ton about it, like the specifics— but I just started using NewsBlur, and I'm just enjoying reading through it. I had an issue with Fiery Feeds on my phone earlier in the year where my entire feed disappeared, and so I got entirely turned off from restarting my feed list... but here I am once again.





I don't really have a point to this post, and I'll be occupied and away from the website for a while just because of finals for my first semester at university, but I wanted to say something, and this was that something :)

Now all I have to do is get an RSS feed running on this website, and I'll be happy (or happier, whatever).
