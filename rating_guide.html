guide to the rating scale

Whether it is obvious or not, rating scales are very hard to intrepret as a reader, and to uphold as a writer. On top of that, you have to compete with the ideology of every other reviewer on the face of the Earth, which just adds way too many variables into the equation to be worth spending so much time on a rating system.

Which is why I spent the time to work on a rating system...

So here is mine:

masterpiece

This may be the most contentious of this whole scale, since it's so utterly subjective.

In essence, this is something that I feel, despite any nagging shortcomings or slight mishaps, is too great a work on its own, and yields too much influence over me or some subject matter, and thus does not deserve any other rating. However, this exclusively does not include something that just does its job, or feels nice to read. It has to have more nuance, more character, and a bit of soul to itself. It essentially stands on a plane of its own, as something to look up to and admire.

truly transcendent, genre-defining, and/or incredibly moving pieces of work that have influenced even who I am as person

this is a completely different ballpark, and wayy more subjective; it is essentially a personal piece that shows what I love and appreciate most with specific art
